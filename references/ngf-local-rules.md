# NGF Local League Rules — Navajo Girls Fastpitch (2026 Spring Season)

These are the official Navajo Girls Fastpitch (NGF) league rules for the 2026 Spring recreational season and Tournament of Champions (TOC). NGF rules take precedence over USA Softball rules where they conflict.

Source: NGF Rules and Regulations (Revised March 2026) and NGF 2026 Spring TOC Rules.

---

## Rule Hierarchy

NGF rules govern all league play. Where NGF rules are silent, USA Softball Fast Pitch rules apply. NGF rules take precedence over USA Softball rules when they conflict. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.B)

## Game Rules — All Divisions (8U–14U)

### Time Limits

- No new inning shall start after 1 hour and 20 minutes (1:20) of play. (NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)
- Drop dead time is 1:35. If an inning has not ended by 1:35, the game ends and the score reverts to the last completed inning. (Appendices B–E)
- **14U exception:** If the game is tied at the conclusion of the inning or at the 1:35 drop dead time limit, the game ends in a tie during regular season play. (Appendix E)

### Score Reversion Rules (After 1:20)

For all divisions (8U–14U):

- Away team batting and ahead by 5+ runs → game ends, score reverts to previous inning.
- Home team batting and ahead by 1+ runs → game ends, score is final.
- Visiting team at bat and leading by 4 or fewer runs → home team still bats.
- Game tied at conclusion of inning → tie stands. (14U: tie also stands at drop dead time.)

(NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)

### Run Limit Per Inning

Maximum 4 runs per inning. No open innings in any division (8U–14U). (NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)

### Score Reporting

Scores must be reported by **both teams** within 24 hours to scores@navajosoftball.com. If no score is reported, both teams forfeit (double 0-0 loss recorded). (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.A.5)

### Home Team Dugout

Home team occupies the **third base dugout**. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.E.10)

### Ties

Games can end in a tie during regular season play. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.A.4)

## Pitching Rules

### Weekly Pitching Limits

| Division | Innings/Week |
| --- | --- |
| 8U | 6 innings/week |
| 10U | 7 innings/week |
| 12U | 8 innings/week |
| 14U | 8 innings/week |

Pitching to even one batter counts as an inning pitched. (NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)

### Minimum Pitchers Per Game

Each team must use a minimum of 2 pitchers per game. Each pitcher must start and complete one full inning. Failure results in forfeit. (NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)

### 8U — Walks and Coach Pitch Rules

- Walks (base on balls) are allowed only **after April 2nd**, except when bases are loaded. (Appendix B)
- After 4 balls, a coach pitches with the assumed strike count.
- Coach must pitch from the rubber inside the pitching circle; pitch should mimic pitcher speed.
- Maximum 4 coach pitches. After April 2nd, coach pitch is allowed **only** when bases are loaded.
- Bunting is NOT allowed off a coach pitch.
- Batter assumes the strike count. Umpire continues to call balls and strikes. Batter may take pitches called "balls" but is out after 4 pitches or a strikeout. If the 4th pitch is fouled, the at-bat continues.

(NGF Rules & Regs, Appendix B)

## Players and Substitutes

### Minimum Defensive Innings Per Player

Each player shall play a minimum of **2 defensive innings** per game. Exception: if the game lasts fewer than 4 complete innings, each player plays at least 1 defensive inning.

**Penalty:** Loss of the game if all players fail to play minimum innings. If both teams fail, a double loss is declared.

(NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.I.1)

### Infield Requirement Per Player

| Division | Minimum |
| --- | --- |
| 8U | 1 infield inning AND 1 outfield inning (TOC: 1 infield only) |
| 10U | 1 infield inning |
| 12U | 1 infield inning |
| 14U | 1 infield inning |

(NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)

### Max Innings on Bench

Players may not sit out more than **2 innings** per game (all divisions). (NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E; Section XIII.E.1)

### Batting Order

The batting lineup shall consist of all players (entire roster bats). It is encouraged that the batting order fluctuate during the season. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.I.1.d; Appendices B–E)

### Number of Fielders

| Division | Fielders | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 8U | 10 | 4 outfielders (LF, LC, RC, RF) must start in the outfield grass |
| 10U | 9 | |
| 12U | 9 | |
| 14U | 9 | |

(NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B–E)

### Catching Limit

Players may not play the catching position more than **2 consecutive innings** (8U and 10U). (NGF Rules & Regs, Appendices B, C)

### Player Arriving Late / Leaving Early

- Player arriving late is inserted in the **last spot** of the batting lineup. (Section XIII.E.3)
- Player leaving a game early will **not** be recorded as an out when her batting spot comes up. (Section XIII.E.2)
- Player leaving due to injury or illness will NOT be called out during her turn at bat. (Appendices B–E)

### Free Defensive Substitutions

Free defensive substitutions are permitted for all defensive players, including pitchers, provided the minimum playing rule and pitching limitations are followed. (Section XIII.I.1.c)

### Shared / Substitute Players

- Teams unable to field minimum players (10 for 8U; 9 for 10U/12U/14U) may bring in substitute players from the same division or one division lower. These substitutes must bat last and play outfield the entire game. (Section XIII.E.6)
- If a team lacks its ranked pitcher and no other pitcher is present: a pitcher from another team in the same division may substitute and pitch a maximum of 2 innings. That pitcher must bat last and play outfield for the remainder of the game. **Not available during TOC.** (Section XIII.E.4)
- If a team lacks its ranked catcher and no capable catcher is present: a catcher from another team in the same division may substitute with no inning limit. That catcher must bat last and play outfield when not catching. **Not available during TOC.** (Section XIII.E.5)
- If a team already has the minimum number of regular players AND uses a substitute pitcher/catcher: the substitute may only play their needed position (pitcher or catcher) and must sit the bench otherwise — may not play outfield in this scenario. (Section XIII.E.7)
- A shared player cannot substitute for an ejected player. (Section XIII.I.1.f)

### Base Coaches

Players are **not** allowed to base coach, **except in 14U**. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.I.1.g)

### Ejected Players

A player ejected from the game serves a bench ejection — removed from the lineup but may remain in the dugout at the umpire's discretion. An out is recorded each time the ejected player's batting spot comes up for the remainder of the game. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XI.I.4)

## Division-Specific Rules

### 6U Minis (Appendix A1)

- Game duration: maximum 60 minutes.
- Entire lineup bats each inning. Batting order rotates each inning so a different player leads off.
- **First half of season:** Players hit off a tee.
- **Second half of season:** Coach tosses up to 4 pitches; batter may still use the tee if pitches are not hit.
- If a player is called out, the runner remains on base.
- After hitting, batter runs to first base and stays until the next player bats. **No stealing; no advancing on an overthrown ball.**
- Players run the bases regardless of whether they are "out."
- Last batter each inning circles all bases without stopping. Offense informs defense when last batter comes up.
- One coach may be at home plate to assist batters. Base coaches may be positioned next to first and third base.
- Defense: Entire lineup plays the field each inning. Normal infield positions assigned; remaining players in outfield (must start beyond the base path). Defensive lineup rotates each inning. Two coaches may be on the field. As the final batter rounds the bases, defense throws to catcher to attempt tag-outs.

### 6U Super Sixers (Appendix A2)

A hybrid instructional division introducing more 8U rules.

- Drop-dead game time of 1 hour.
- Entire lineup bats each inning. 10" RIF 1 softball.
- Catcher position played; a coach may assist the catcher.

**Offensive Rules — First Half of Season:**

- Manager/coach tosses 4 pitches to own batter. If not hit, tee is provided.
- No called outs.
- Batter can run to first on a hit; teams bat through entire lineup each inning.
- Only USA Softball registered managers/coaches may assist a batter during the at-bat.

**Offensive Rules — Second Half of Season:**

- Player pitches (up to 2 pitches). If batter does not hit, coach tosses 3 pitches (foul balls on/after the 3rd pitch not counted). If still not hit, tee is provided.
- A defensive coach/assistant may be stationed by the pitcher to help.
- Base runners may only take one base on an overthrow.
- Base runners cannot steal home.
- Runners may run two bases if the ball is hit to the outfield.

### 8U (Appendix B)

Key differences from standard USA Softball and from other NGF divisions:

- 10 fielders (4 outfielders — LF, LC, RC, RF — must start in the outfield grass)
- Max 4 runs/inning; no open innings
- No new inning at 1:20; drop dead at 1:35
- 6 innings/week pitching limit
- 2 pitchers required per game (each must complete 1 full inning)
- Walks not allowed before April 2nd (except bases loaded); coach pitch rules apply — see Pitching Rules section above
- Stealing allowed (1 base per pitch); stealing **home NOT allowed**
- No infield fly; no dropped 3rd strike; no continuation play
- Advance on overthrow: 1 base, ball dead
- All bat entire roster; max 2 innings on bench
- Minimum: 1 infield AND 1 outfield inning per player
- Catching: max 2 consecutive innings

### 10U (Appendix C)

- 9 fielders
- Max 4 runs/inning; no open innings
- No new inning at 1:20; drop dead at 1:35
- 7 innings/week pitching limit
- 2 pitchers required per game (each must complete 1 full inning)
- Stealing allowed; stealing **home allowed** (home plate is open/hot)
- Continuation play allowed
- Infield fly allowed (umpire's discretion); dropped 3rd strike allowed
- Advance on overthrow allowed
- All bat entire roster; max 2 innings on bench
- Minimum: 1 infield inning per player
- Catching: max 2 consecutive innings

### 12U (Appendix D)

- 9 fielders
- Max 4 runs/inning; no open innings
- No new inning at 1:20; drop dead at 1:35
- 8 innings/week pitching limit
- 2 pitchers required per game (each must complete 1 full inning)
- Stealing allowed; stealing **home allowed** (home plate is open/hot)
- Continuation play allowed
- Infield fly allowed (umpire's discretion); dropped 3rd strike allowed
- Advance on overthrow allowed
- All bat entire roster; max 2 innings on bench
- Minimum: 1 infield inning per player
- **Fielder's mask optional for outfielders** (required for all infield positions)

### 14U (Appendix E)

- 9 fielders
- Max 4 runs/inning; no open innings
- No new inning at 1:20; drop dead at 1:35
- 8 innings/week pitching limit
- 2 pitchers required per game (each must complete 1 full inning)
- Stealing allowed; stealing **home allowed** (home plate is open/hot)
- Continuation play allowed
- Infield fly allowed (umpire's discretion); dropped 3rd strike allowed
- Advance on overthrow allowed
- All bat entire roster; max 2 innings on bench
- Minimum: 1 infield inning per player
- **Players may base coach** (exception for 14U)
- **Metal cleats permitted** (exception for 14U)
- **Fielder's mask optional for outfielders**
- Tied game at 1:35 drop dead ends in a tie (regular season)

## Tournament of Champions (TOC) Rules

Source: NGF 2026 Spring TOC Rules.

The TOC is a double-elimination tournament at the conclusion of the regular season for 8U, 10U, 12U, and 14U. Regular season rules remain in effect unless amended below.

### General TOC Rules (All Divisions)

- **No substitute/pickup players.** Teams play with their regular season roster only. (NGF Rules & Regs, Section XIII.E.8)
- **Home team** is determined by regular season standings (higher seed). Home team occupies the third base dugout.
- **Line-up exchange:** Before the game, coaches must exchange defensive line-ups listing each player's name, jersey number, and defensive assignment through 4 innings (or enough to demonstrate infield play by all players).
- **Time:** No new inning after 1:20. If an inning starts before 1:20, it must be completed. **There is no drop dead time** (unlike regular season).
- **Ties:** International Tiebreaker (ITB) rule — the last scheduled batter in that inning starts on second base with no outs. All games are played until a winner is determined. No ties in TOC.
- **Mercy rule:** 12 runs ahead after 4 innings; 8 runs ahead after 5 innings.
- **Pitching:** Regular season pitching rules apply. Must have 2 different pitchers pitch at least 1 complete inning each to avoid forfeit.
- **Extended games:** If a game goes more than 6 innings, players may sit out more than 2 innings (the regular-season 2-inning bench limit is suspended).
- **Scorecard:** The winning team must submit scorecard information to scores@navajosoftball.com on the same day by 9:00 PM. Scorecard must include the final score and innings pitched by each pitcher on both teams.
- **Infield requirement (8U):** 1 infield inning only (regular season requires 1 infield AND 1 outfield).
- **Championship game seeding:** The team from the winner's bracket is the home team and occupies the 3rd base dugout.
- **Pitchers' innings reset** for the first championship game.

### 8U TOC Rules

- Minimum 8 players to start; forfeit if fewer.
- Pitchers allowed **6 innings for every 2 consecutive games played**.
- All players must play 1 infield position. No outfield requirement.
- **Walks:** Players may walk freely until bases are loaded. Once bases are loaded, coach assumes strike count after 4 balls on the batter. Umpires continue to call strikes on coach pitch; maximum 4 pitches (if 4th pitch is fouled, at-bat continues).
- If a batter is hit by a pitch, she takes her base, even if this results in a run scored.
- If the pitching coach is hit by a batted ball: play is ruled dead, all runners return to their bases, no pitch is called, batter continues at-bat.
- **No run limit in the 5th inning and later** (4-run limit still applies through 4 innings).
- Championship/if-necessary game: no new inning after 1:20.

### 10U TOC Rules

- Minimum 8 players to start; forfeit if fewer.
- No substitute/pickup players. A team playing with 8 players must take an out when the ninth batter would be up.
- Pitchers allowed **7 innings for every 2 consecutive games played**.
- **No run limit in the 4th inning and later** (4-run limit still applies through 3 innings).
- Championship/if-necessary game: no new inning after 1:20.

### 12U TOC Rules

- Minimum 8 players to start; forfeit if fewer.
- No substitute/pickup players. A team playing with 8 players must take an out when the ninth batter would be up.
- Pitchers allowed **8 innings for every 2 consecutive games played**.
- **No run limit in the 4th inning and later** (4-run limit still applies through 3 innings).
- Championship/if-necessary game: no new inning after 1:20.

### 14U TOC Rules

No 14U-specific TOC rules are listed separately. General TOC rules above apply.
