# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 6A: Pitching (Fast Pitch)

*This rule applies to Junior Olympic Girls Fast Pitch (12U). Rules for Slow Pitch (6B-6D) are not covered here.*

---

## Section 1: Pitching Position (Starting Position)

**A.** The pitcher must take a position with:
- Both feet on the ground and in contact with the pitcher's plate
- The pivot foot in contact with the pitcher's plate (see definition)
- Shoulders in line with first and third base
- Ball held in both hands, in front of the body, for at least one (1) second and not more than ten (10) seconds

**B.** The pitcher must face the batter to begin the pitching motion.

**C.** Both male and female pitchers must have their pivot foot in contact with the pitcher's plate.

**D.** Both female and male pitchers must take a position with the non-pivot foot on or behind the pitcher's plate.

**E.** The pitcher's plate must be occupied by the pitcher during the pitching motion.

---

## Section 2: Warm-Up Pitches

**A.** At the start of each half-inning, the pitcher is allowed five (5) warm-up pitches (or one minute, whichever comes first).

**B.** After a pitcher enters a game mid-inning (replacing another pitcher), they are allowed the same warm-up pitches.

**C.** If an injury to the pitcher occurs during an inning, the umpire may allow warm-up pitches at their discretion.

**D.** A catcher must receive the warm-up pitches. If the catcher is not available, a coach or player may catch them.

---

## Section 3: Legal Delivery (How the Pitch Must Be Made)

The following must all be observed for a pitch to be legal:

**A. Preliminary:** The pitcher must take a signal (or simulate taking a signal) from the catcher while in contact with the pitcher's plate. The ball must be held in the hand or glove during the signal.

**B. Hands together:** After taking the signal, the pitcher must bring their hands together for at least one (1) second and not more than ten (10) seconds before starting the pitching motion.

**C. Start of pitch:** The pitch begins when the pitcher takes one hand off the ball after bringing the hands together.

**D. Step:** The pitcher must take **one step** only, which must be toward home plate, simultaneous with the release of the ball.
- **Male Adult and JO Boys** pitchers no longer need to step forward within the 24-inch length of the pitcher's plate.
- For women/girls: Pushing off from a spot other than the pitcher's plate is a crow hop and is illegal.
- Dragging or pushing off with the pivot foot is required. Pushing from any spot other than the pitcher's plate is a crow hop.

**E. Delivery:** The delivery shall be an underhand motion with the hand below the hip.
- The wrist shall be no farther from the body than the elbow
- The ball must pass by the body before the release
- The delivery shall be in a smooth, continuous motion
- No reversal or stop is permitted
- The pitch may not be delivered behind the back or between the legs

**F. Release:** The ball must be released simultaneously with the forward step.

**G. Crow Hop (illegal):** A crow hop occurs when the pitcher:
1. Slides the pivot foot forward, not in contact with the pitcher's plate
2. Lifts the pivot foot and steps forward before releasing the ball
3. Jumps forward from the pitcher's plate with the pivot foot prior to starting the pitch

**H. Leaping (Fast Pitch):** In Women's and JO Fast Pitch divisions, **leaping is allowed** — both feet may become airborne on the initial move as the pitcher pushes off the pitcher's plate — **as long as the pivot foot toes are pointed downward toward the ground.** If the pivot foot toes are not pointed downward, an illegal pitch shall be called.

**I.** Pitchers may legally push off and drag the pivot foot on the ground during the step. A pitcher sliding their foot across the pitcher's plate is legal. A pitcher whose foot rolls over is not considered a step.

---

## Section 4: Pitching Requirements

No specific additional requirements beyond Section 3.

---

## Section 5: Illegal Pitches

The following are illegal pitches (each is a ball charged to the pitcher; batters and runners may advance but play continues — delayed dead ball):

1. Not taking or simulating a signal from the catcher
2. Making more than one revolution of the arm in the wind-up (two full revolutions is legal; three is not)
3. Stepping toward a base rather than toward home plate
4. Making a motion to pitch and not completing the pitch
5. Removing the pivot foot from contact with the pitcher's plate before releasing the ball (other than a legal leaping motion)
6. Taking more than one step
7. Delivering the ball with the hand or wrist above the hip or elbow (wrist constraint)
8. Delivering a crow hop (illegal)
9. Delivering a quick return pitch (see Section 10)
10. Delivering the ball with a stop or reversal of the forward motion (no "hitch" or hesitation)
11. Making a pitch while not in contact with the pitcher's plate

**Effect of an illegal pitch:**
- The ball is dead immediately only if a hit batter or wild pitch results
- Otherwise: delayed dead ball is signaled
- If the batter hits the ball and all runners advance at least one base on the play, the illegal pitch is disregarded
- If the batter does not swing, a ball is called and all runners advance one base
- The batter may also swing at an illegal pitch

---

## Section 6: Foreign Substance

**A.** The pitcher may not place a foreign substance on the ball or pitching hand. The ball shall be removed from the game if found to have a foreign substance on it.

**B.** Under the supervision and control of the umpire, powder resin or an approved drying agent (on a USA Softball list) may be used on the pitcher's hand. The resin must be left on the ground and the approved drying agent must be in the pitcher's pocket.

**C.** The pitcher may not wear a batting glove on the pitching hand.

---

## Section 7: Pitcher Returning to Pitch in Same Half-Inning

A pitcher returning to pitch in the same half-inning shall not be given warm-up pitches, unless an injury has occurred in the interim. There is no limit on the number of times a player can return to the pitching position in a game, as long as they have not left the batting order (which is governed by substitution rules).

---

## Section 8: No-Pitch Situations

No pitch shall be declared when:
1. The batter steps in the box while the ball is dead
2. The pitcher pitches before the umpire calls "play ball" or before the batter is ready
3. The pitcher pitches while a fielder is out of position
4. A quick return pitch is recognized
5. Time is called for any reason before the pitch is released

---

## Section 9: Warm-Up Pitches Between Innings

Warm-up pitches are allowed between innings, as described in Section 2.

---

## Section 10: Quick Return Pitch

A quick return pitch is a pitch made with the obvious intent to catch the batter off-balance (i.e., before the batter is ready). This is an illegal pitch. Effect: ball is called.

---

## Section 11: Illegal Pitch Penalty Summary

- If no batter action: ball charged to pitcher
- If batter swings and misses: strike (no ball awarded)
- If batter hits safely and all runners advance: disregard the illegal pitch
- If batter hits and some runners don't advance: enforce whichever is more beneficial to the offense

---

## Section 12: Time Allowed to Pitch

The pitcher has **10 seconds** to release the ball after receiving it from the catcher (Fast Pitch). The 10-second clock starts when the pitcher has the ball and the batter is in the box. If the pitcher fails to release within 10 seconds, a ball is called.
