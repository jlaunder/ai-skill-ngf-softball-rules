# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 8: Batter-Runner and Runner

*This is the most complex rule in softball. Applies to Fast Pitch / JO Girls 12U.*

---

## Section 1: Batter-Runner — Awarded First Base

The batter becomes a batter-runner and is awarded first base (without liability to be put out) when:

**A.** A fair ball is hit (the batter-runner must run to first base).

**B.** The batter receives a base on balls (four balls = walk). In Fast Pitch, all four pitches must be thrown.

**C.** The batter is hit by a pitched ball (hit by pitch), provided they did not swing and attempted to avoid it.

**D.** The catcher commits catcher's obstruction (interference) with the batter's swing.
- Effect: Delayed dead ball. If the batter reaches base safely and all runners advance at least one base, the obstruction is disregarded. Otherwise, the batter is awarded first base and all runners advance one base.

**E.** The catcher (or any fielder) uses detached equipment to touch the ball:
- The batter is awarded 3 bases (not just 1)

**F.** Dropped Third Strike (Fast Pitch):
- The batter-runner is **not** called out on a dropped third strike and may attempt to advance to first base when:
  1. The catcher fails to catch the third strike, AND
  2. **First base is unoccupied** (at the time of the pitch), OR **There are two outs**
- If first base is occupied with fewer than two outs, the batter is automatically out on the third strike regardless of whether the catcher catches it.
- The batter is put out by being tagged or thrown out at first base.
- Effect: If the batter-runner is out, it's a strikeout. The ball is live during a dropped third strike (runners may advance at their risk).

---

## Section 2: Batter-Runner Running to First Base

**A.** The batter-runner must advance to first base after a fair ball.

**B.** If first base is unoccupied and a fair ball is hit, the batter-runner must advance.

**C.** The batter-runner is out if they run more than 3 feet from a direct line to first base to avoid a tag, unless doing so avoids interfering with a fielder fielding the ball.

**D.** Over-running first base (see Rules Supplement #37): After reaching first base, the batter-runner may turn either left or right when returning to first. If any attempt to advance to second is made (by direction of turn or body language), they may be tagged out.

**E. Three-foot running lane:** The batter-runner must run within the three-foot lane (the last half of the baseline to first base) when a throw is being made from a fielder to first base. The inside line of the three-foot lane is an extension of the first base line. If the batter-runner interferes with the throw while outside the lane, they are out.
- Exception: The batter-runner may use the fair territory side (white portion) of the double base to avoid interference.

**F.** Running back to first base: After a fly ball is caught, the batter-runner who did not touch first base must return and retouch first base before being tagged or thrown out (appeal play).

**G.** Infield fly: When an infield fly is declared, the batter is out immediately. The ball is live. Runners may advance at their own risk but are not required to.

**H.** Home run: On a fair ball over the fence, the batter-runner is awarded a home run and must touch all bases in order. Runners already on base also score.

**I.** Double base/first base: On a batted ball, the batter-runner must use the colored (foul territory) portion of the double base when a play is being made at first base. The fielder must use the white (fair territory) portion. If both use the correct portion, no collision issue exists.

**J.** Overthrow / errant throw: On an errant or missed throw that pulls the fielder into foul territory, the batter-runner may use either portion (white or orange) of the double base.

**N.** Double base rule — Interference: If the batter-runner and the fielder collide while each using the correct portion of the double base, no interference or obstruction is called.

---

## Section 2 (continued): Batter-Runner Is Out

The batter-runner is out when:

**A.** A fielder holds the ball while touching first base before the batter-runner arrives.

**B.** A fielder with the ball tags the batter-runner before they reach first base.

**C.** Another runner is forced out at any base due to the batter becoming a batter-runner (force play — see force out definition).

**D.** A fielder catches a fly ball in fair or foul territory before it touches the ground.

**E.** The batter-runner runs outside the three-foot lane and interferes with a throw to first base.

**F.** The batter-runner intentionally interferes with a fielder fielding a batted ball.

**G.** On a force play, a fielder with the ball touches the base ahead of the batter-runner.

**H.** Batter-runner and an infielder collide while the infielder was fielding a batted ball and the batter-runner interfered (not applicable if infielder was in the base path without the ball).

**I.** The infield fly is declared — batter is automatically out.

**K.** The batter runs back toward home plate to avoid a tag and touches home plate — batter-runner is out.

## Section 3: Base Runners — Same Base Occupied

**A.** Two runners may not occupy the same base at the same time. If they do, the trailing (following) runner is out if tagged while two runners are on the same base.

**B.** Exception: If the lead runner is forced to advance and cannot return (forced off the base), the trailing runner has the right to the base and the lead runner is the one who can be put out.

**C.** If a forced runner is tagged out, the force is removed for all preceding runners.

**D.** Runners always run in the proper order: 1B → 2B → 3B → Home.

**E.** Effect: Two runners occupying the same base — the following runner is the one who may be tagged out if both are simultaneously touching the base.

---

## Section 4: Stealing (Fast Pitch)

**A.** In Fast Pitch, **runners may steal** (advance to the next base) at any time. The runner may not leave the base before the pitcher releases the ball.

**B.** A runner who leaves the base before the ball is released is called back (or out if tagged).

**C.** On a walk (base on balls), runners are awarded one base. If the walk forces a runner home, that run scores without play.

---

## Section 5: Runner Is Out

A runner is out when:

**A.** Tagged with the ball while off base.

**B.** A force play is completed (fielder with ball touches the base to which the runner is forced).

**C.** The runner runs more than 3 feet from a direct line between bases to avoid a tag (unless it's to avoid interfering with a fielder fielding a ball).

**D.** The runner is hit by a fair batted ball while off a base and before the ball passes an infielder (other than the pitcher). Exception: if an infielder touched the ball first and the runner is hit after that, the ball is live and the runner is not out.

**E.** The runner abandons the base and leaves the playing field while the ball is in play.

**F.** The runner deliberately interferes with a batted or thrown ball.

**G.** The runner passes a preceding runner who has not been put out — the runner who passed is out.

**H.** The runner is called out on a force play and a preceding runner has already scored — the run does not count if the force out is the third out of the inning.

**I.** The runner fails to return to their base after a fly ball is caught (must tag up before advancing) — out on appeal.

**J.** The runner interferes with a fielder attempting to make a play.

**K.** A runner is struck by a fair batted ball while standing on a base and the closest defensive player was in front of the base — the runner is out.

**L.** The runner intentionally contacts the ball with their hands while running the bases.

**M.** The runner slides headfirst into a base (some associations — not standard in JO Girls).

**N.** The runner misses a base (on appeal — see Section 7).

**P.** The runner deliberately crashes into a fielder holding the ball to break up a play (see Rules Supplement #13).

**Q.** The runner leaves the base early in Fast Pitch (before the pitcher releases the ball).

**R.** A runner leaving base too soon on a caught fly ball (on appeal — see Section 7 and RS #1).

**S.** A runner who leaves the base too soon when the ball is pitched (Fast Pitch) — runner called out on appeal.

**T.** The Look Back Rule — runner stopped or reversed after pitcher has ball in circle (see Section 10 and RS #34).

**U.** Runner abandons the base.

**V.** Running start — in Fast Pitch, a runner who gets a running start before the pitcher releases the ball.

**X.** Runner found using an illegal bat — runner is called out.

**Y.** Runners on the same base — after a "time out," two or more runners change positions and the manager is ejected.

---

## Section 6: Fly Ball — Tag Up

**A.** After a fly ball is caught by a fielder, a runner may advance **only after** the fielder first touches (catches) the fly ball.

**B.** The runner must **tag up** — physically touch and remain in contact with their base until after the fly ball is first touched by the fielder. Then they may attempt to advance.

**C.** A runner who advances before the catch may be put out by an appeal (throw to the base the runner left early, or tag the runner, before the next legal pitch).

**D.** On a dropped fly ball (not caught), runners may advance freely.

---

## Section 7: Awards — Base Awards and Overthrows

### Awarded Bases (Free Bases)

**A. Runners advancing on a wild pitch or passed ball:** runners advance at their own risk (no base award — they must reach safely).

**B. Interference by fielder on a batted ball:** if a fielder obstructs a batter-runner, the batter-runner is awarded at least the base they were running toward.

**C. Runner obstructed (obstruction by fielder):** obstructed runner is awarded the base they would have reached had obstruction not occurred (at umpire's discretion). See RS #36.

**D. Overthrows — award on batted balls (fair territory):**
- When a fair ball is thrown out of play (into dead ball territory), all runners (including the batter-runner) are awarded **two bases** from the last base legally touched **at the time the ball was thrown**.
- Exception: If the ball is thrown from the outfield and goes out of play, runners are awarded 2 bases from the base occupied at the time of the throw.

**E. Overthrows — award on pitched balls (Fast Pitch wild pitch or passed ball):**
- On a pitched ball that goes out of play (wild pitch or passed ball), runners are awarded **one base** from the base legally occupied at the time of the pitch.

**F. Overthrows from the pitcher's plate to first base:** 2 bases if the ball goes out of play; 1 base if it stays in play.

**G. Award on blocked ball (batted fair):** 2 bases.

**H. Overthrow — exception (outfield throw):**
- When the first throw by an **outfielder** goes out of play while a runner is between 1B and 2B (or between 2B and 3B), the award is from the base occupied **at the time of the pitch** (not at the time of the throw).

**I. Infield fly:** Batter-runner is automatically out; ball is live; runners may advance at their own risk.

**J. Force out on overthrow:** See RS #21.

**K. Infield fly intentionally dropped:** Ball is dead; batter-runner is out; all other runners return to last base occupied at the time of the pitch.

## Section 8: Overrunning First Base

*(See Rules Supplement #37)*

**A.** A batter-runner may overrun first base without being tagged out, provided they immediately return to first base without making a turn toward second base.

**B.** If a batter-runner overruns first base and makes any attempt to advance (by turning toward second base), they may be tagged out.

**C.** Using the double base: When overrunning first base and returning, the batter-runner may use either the white or orange portion of the base. On an errant or missed throw that pulls the fielder into foul territory, the batter-runner may use either portion.

---

## Section 9: Courtesy Runner

*(See Rule 4 and Rules Supplement #12)*

**Fast Pitch (including 12U JO Girls):**

**A.** A courtesy runner may be used for the **pitcher and/or catcher only**, at any time.

**B.** The courtesy runner must be a player who is **not currently in the batting order**. In JO Pool Play Only, any player may be a courtesy runner.

**C.** No charged substitution is made. The pitcher and catcher remain in their positions.

**D.** A player may serve as a courtesy runner **only once per inning**. If used as a courtesy runner a second time in the same inning, they are disqualified.

**E.** The courtesy runner becomes subject to all rules for runners once on base.

**F.** If an unreported courtesy runner is discovered (they are on base but were not reported to the plate umpire), the runner is disqualified when the infraction is brought to the umpire's attention by the offended team. The runner is removed from base.

---

## Section 10: Look Back Rule (Fast Pitch)

*(See also Rules Supplement #34)*

**Once the pitcher has possession of the ball within the 8-foot circle AND the batter-runner has touched or passed first base and a play has ended**, all runners must immediately:
- Advance to the next base, OR
- Return to the last base occupied

**If a runner stops or reverses direction after the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the runner is OUT.**

### Key details:

**A.** The Look Back rule does NOT go into effect for any runner until the batter-runner has touched first base or been called out, AND the pitcher has control of the ball within the 8-foot circle.

**B.** If a runner fails to immediately advance or return after the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the runner is called out.

**C.** Exception — the runner is NOT called out when:
1. A play is being made on another runner
2. The pitcher leaves the circle or drops the ball
3. The pitcher releases the ball to the batter (next pitch)
4. The pitcher places the ball under their arm or between their legs to free both hands (the ball is not considered "controlled" in these cases)

**D.** A base on balls (walk) or dropped third strike: Runners may advance until the batter-runner reaches first base. Once the batter-runner touches first base and the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the Look Back rule applies.

**E.** A runner moving toward a base in good faith is not subject to the Look Back rule. Making an attempt or fake justifies calling the runner out.

**F.** If multiple runners are off base and more than one umpire calls a runner out under the Look Back rule, the ball is dead. The first runner declared out remains out; all other runners return to the base they left.

**G.** A batter-runner overrunning first base and then stopping: If the batter-runner stops while overrunning and the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the batter-runner must immediately advance or return. If they stop on first base, they are safe (since they stopped at a base). If they stop between first and second, and the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the Look Back rule applies.

**H.** If a runner is moving toward a base and the pitcher receives the ball in the circle, the runner may stop once and then must continue advancing or return. Making a second stop after already having stopped once is a violation.

**L.** Being in the 8-foot circle: The pitcher is considered in the circle if both feet are within or partially within the lines. The pitcher is not in the circle if either foot is completely outside the lines.

## Section 11: Interference, Obstruction, and Special Plays

*(Detailed analysis — also see Rules Supplement #33 and #36)*

### Interference

**General:** Interference is an offensive act (or team member act) that impedes, hinders, or confuses a defensive player executing a play.

**Runner interference includes:**
- Touching the white portion of the double base on a play at first when they should use the orange portion (interference)
- Running into a fielder attempting to field a batted ball
- Being hit by a fair batted ball while off a base before it passes an infielder
- Deliberately interfering with a thrown ball

**Effect of runner interference:**
- The runner who interfered is out
- The ball is dead
- Other runners return to the last base occupied before the interference

**Batter interference:**
- If the batter interferes with the catcher's throw on an attempted steal, the batter is out and the runner returns to the last base occupied

### Obstruction

**General:** Obstruction is a fielder's act that impedes the progress of a batter-runner or runner who is legally running the bases, when the fielder is not in possession of the ball and not in the act of fielding a batted ball.

**Effect:**
- The umpire calls obstruction immediately and signals a delayed dead ball
- The obstructed runner is awarded the base they would have reached had obstruction not occurred
- Other runners may advance or return based on the umpire's judgment of where they would have been

**Fake tag:** A fake tag is obstruction and must be called. The fielder without the ball simulates a tag to stop or deceive a runner. The obstructed runner is awarded the base they would have reached.

### Catcher Obstruction (interference)

If the catcher obstructs the batter's swing (catcher's interference), a delayed dead ball is called. If the batter reaches base and all runners advance, the obstruction is disregarded. Otherwise, the batter is awarded first base and all runners advance one base.

## Section 7 (Additional): Other Runner Awards

**Force Out:** On a force play, the fielder need only touch the base (not tag the runner) while holding the ball. A force play exists when a runner is required to advance because the batter becomes a batter-runner. When the force is removed (e.g., a preceding runner is put out), the force play no longer applies and the fielder must tag the runner.

**Infield Fly Rule:** When the infield fly is called, the batter is immediately out. The runners are not forced to advance and may remain on their bases at their risk. The runners may attempt to advance but do so at their own risk (they must tag up if the ball is caught, or advance freely if it drops untouched).
