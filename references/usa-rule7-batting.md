# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 7: Batting

---

## Section 1: Batter's Position in the Box

**A.** The batter must stand in the batter's box with both feet entirely inside the lines of the box.
- The lines of the batter's box are considered part of the box (batter may stand on the lines)

**B.** The batter must be in the box and ready to bat within **10 seconds** of the umpire calling "Play Ball."

**C.** *Violation:* A strike may be called if the batter fails to take position in the box within the required time.

**D.** The batter must not leave the box unless:
- Time is called by the umpire
- They attempt to hit the ball
- A pitch is intentionally swung at
- They are forced out by a play

**E.** *Batter out for leaving the box:* If the batter intentionally leaves the box to avoid a pitch, a ball is called. (Not a penalty if the batter steps out due to a pitch that would normally cause them to move out of the way.)

*(See Rules Supplement #5 — Batter Remains in Batter's Box for JO Fast Pitch)*

---

## Section 2: Batting Order

**A.** All players must bat in the batting order that was submitted at the start of the game.

**B.** *Batting out of order:* If a batter bats out of turn (proper order), the correct batter (the one who should have batted) is called out upon appeal. The appeal must be made before the next legal or illegal pitch after the improper batter has completed their turn at bat (reached base, been put out, or scored). Runners return to bases occupied before the improper at-bat. The next batter is whoever follows the player who was called out (the proper batter who should have batted).

**D.** If no appeal is made before the next pitch, the improper at-bat stands and the batting order continues from the improper batter.

---

## Section 3: Strike Zone

**A.** (Fast Pitch) The strike zone is the space over home plate between the batter's armpits and the top of their knees when the batter assumes a natural batting stance.

**B.** The umpire determines whether the pitched ball passes through the strike zone.

**C.** The strike zone is based on the batter's natural stance, not where they crouch or stand.

*(For JO Girls / 12U: No special modification to the standard Fast Pitch strike zone.)*

---

## Section 4: Strikes

A strike is called when:

**A.** A pitched ball passes through any part of the strike zone and the batter does not swing.

**B.** The batter swings at a pitched ball and misses (regardless of whether it's in the strike zone).

**C.** A foul ball is hit when the batter has fewer than two strikes.

**D.** A foul tip is caught by the catcher (counts as a strike in all cases, including with two strikes — batter is out on a caught foul tip with two strikes).

**E.** A bunt attempt results in a foul ball (even on the third strike — batter is out if a bunt attempt is fouled on the third strike in Fast Pitch).

**F.** A ball is hit while the batter is outside the batter's box (struck ball outside the box).

**G.** The batter is called out on strikes — three strikes = strikeout = batter is out (unless dropped third strike rule applies — see Section 6 and Rule 8, Section 1).

---

## Section 5: Balls

A ball is called when:

**A.** A pitch does not pass through the strike zone and the batter does not swing.

**B.** An illegal pitch is delivered (see Rule 6A, Section 5).

**C.** The pitcher delivers a pitch before the batter is ready (or outside the 10-second limit).

**D.** A pitched ball hits the batter — the batter is awarded first base (see Section 6 below for hit by pitch).

**E.** The pitcher makes an illegal pitch.

Four balls = base on balls (walk) = batter awarded first base.

---

## Section 6: Batter Is Out

The batter is out when:

**A.** A fair or foul fly ball is caught by a fielder before touching the ground.

**B.** A third strike is legally caught by the catcher (strikeout). Exception: dropped third strike — see Rule 8, Section 1.

**C.** A bunt attempt is fouled on the third strike.

**D.** The batter fails to take position in the batter's box within the required time (after repeated warnings).

**E.** A third strike is not caught but first base is occupied with fewer than two outs — the batter is out and may not attempt to advance to first base.

**F.** The batter enters the batter's box with an illegal bat and is discovered before or during the at-bat.
- Effect A: If discovered before a pitch, the illegal bat is removed.
- Effect B: If discovered after a pitch but before the next pitch, the batter is out and any runners return to the base they occupied before the at-bat.

**G.** The batter interferes with the catcher catching a fly ball.

**H.** The batter uses a bat that is altered or not approved (illegal bat).
- Effect: If discovered before the next pitch to the next batter, the batter is out (if reached base), runners return. If not discovered until later, no action is taken.

**I.** The batter intentionally hits a fair batted ball a second time.
- Effect: The ball is dead, the batter is out, and runners return.

**J.** The batter illegally bats the ball (hits the ball while stepping out of the box with one or both feet on the ground outside the box lines).

**K.** The batter strikes out and the catcher catches the third strike — the batter is out.

**M.** A fair ball strikes the batter while in the batter's box — batter is out, ball is dead.

**N.** The on-deck batter interferes with the catcher or a play at home plate.

**R-U.** The batter interferes with the catcher's throw on an attempted steal.

---

## Section 7: Hit By Pitch

**A.** If a pitched ball hits the batter (or their clothing), the batter is awarded first base, provided:
1. The batter attempted to avoid being hit (or the pitch is inside — the batter doesn't need to make extraordinary efforts to avoid it)
2. The batter did not swing at the pitch

**B.** If the batter is hit by a pitch but swings and makes contact, the swing takes precedence. If the ball is fair, it's a batted ball. If foul, it's a foul.

**C.** If the batter makes no attempt to avoid a pitch that hits them (leans into it), the umpire may not award first base and call it a ball instead.

---

## Rules Supplement Relevant to Batting

- **RS #5 (Batter Remains in Box — JO Fast Pitch):** The rule is not intended to penalize the player; it speeds up the game. The batter may still get a signal with one foot in the box and take practice swings. If the batter leaves the box illegally, a warning should be given. A strike should not be called without a warning. One, two, or three strikes may be called on the same batter under this ruling.

- **RS #6 (Batting Order Is Official):** The batting order is official when reviewed by the manager and the plate umpire at the Pre-Game Meeting. Once approved, any changes are substitutions.

- **RS #7 (Batting Out of the Batter's Box):** To call a batter out for batting out of the box, one foot or both feet must be completely outside the lines of the batter's box when contact is made with the ball. The lines are inside the box. When in doubt, benefit goes to the batter.

- **RS #10 (Check Swing / Bunt Strike):** Four areas determine whether a batter swung: (1) Did the batter roll their wrists? (2) Did they swing through the ball and bring the bat back? (3) Was the bat out in front of the body? (4) Did they make an attempt to hit the pitch? On a bunt, was the bat moved toward the ball? If in doubt, the plate umpire calls it. The catcher may ask the plate umpire to ask a base umpire for a check swing ruling.
