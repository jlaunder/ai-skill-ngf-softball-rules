# USA Softball 2026 — Rules 9–12: Protests, Umpires, Scoring, Use of Rules

---

## Rule 9: Protests

### Section 1: Types of Protests

**A.** A protest may be made only on matters of rule interpretation. Judgment calls (ball/strike, fair/foul, safe/out) may NOT be protested.

**B.** A protest may not be made on a misapplication of a rule that was not called (i.e., failure to call a rule violation is not grounds for a protest unless the non-call directly affected the outcome).

### Section 2: Examples of Protestable Situations

**A.** Incorrect application of a penalty for a rule violation.
**B.** Awarding of wrong number of bases on an overthrow.

### Section 3: Protest Procedure

1. The manager must notify the umpire that the game is being played under protest **immediately**, before the next legal or illegal pitch (or before the umpires leave the field if it occurs on the last play of the game).
2. The protest must be made before the next pitch.
3. The manager must state the specific rule that was misapplied.

### Section 4: Notification of Intent to Protest

The manager must declare the protest at the time of the disputed play, before play continues.

### Section 5: Time Limit for Filing

The protest must be submitted in writing to the appropriate governing body within the time limit established by that body (typically 24–48 hours after the game).

### Section 6: Information Required for a Protest

The written protest must include:
- The specific rule in dispute
- The facts of the situation
- What the protesting manager contends the rule should be
- The inning, score, and game situation at the time of the dispute

### Section 7: Decision

The governing body (league board or Umpire in Chief) shall render a decision within a reasonable time. The governing body's decision is final and is not subject to further appeal.

## Rule 10: Umpires

### Section 1: Authority

**A.** The umpire is the sole judge of play on the field. The umpire's judgment on fair/foul, safe/out, balls/strikes is final.

**B.** The umpire has the authority to enforce all rules and to make all decisions.

**C.** Umpires may be one (plate umpire only) or two (plate umpire and base umpire).

### Section 2: Change of Umpires

An umpire may be replaced only in cases of incapacitation or if there is an extreme emergency.

### Section 3: Reversal of Decisions

**B.** An umpire may reverse a decision if the umpire immediately realizes the decision was incorrect, before the next pitch.

**C.** An umpire may not reverse a decision based on an argument by a manager or player.

### Section 4: Interference With Umpires

**D.** If an umpire is hit by a fair batted ball before it passes an infielder (and the infielder had a chance to make a play), the ball is in play (Fast Pitch). The batter-runner is awarded first base only if the umpire's interference prevented an out being made.

**E.** If an umpire interferes with a catcher's throw on a steal attempt, the play is nullified and the runner returns (unless the steal was successful).

**F.** Umpire interference: If a plate umpire interferes with the catcher, it is a delayed dead ball.

### Ejection Authority

The umpire may eject any player, coach, or manager for:
- Arguing a judgment call
- Unsportsmanlike conduct
- Physical contact with an umpire
- Persistent argument after being warned

## Rule 11: Scoring

### Section 1: Official Scorer

The official scorer is responsible for keeping an accurate record of the game.

### Section 2: Box Score

The box score shall include at-bats, runs, hits, errors, and other standard statistics.

### Section 3: Base Hits

A base hit is credited to the batter when:
- The batter reaches base safely because of a fair ball that is not fielded with ordinary effort
- An error or fielder's choice is not involved

An error is charged to a fielder who makes a play that, with ordinary effort, should have retired or held the runner.

### Section 4: Runs Batted In

An RBI is credited to a batter when a runner scores as a result of the batter's hit, sacrifice fly, or other at-bat, unless the run was scored due to an error or double play.

### Section 5: Win Credited to Pitcher

The win is credited to the pitcher who was pitching when their team last took the lead and maintained it through the rest of the game.

### Section 6: Loss Charged to Pitcher

The loss is charged to the pitcher who allowed the opposing team to score the run that gave them a lead they maintained through the rest of the game.

### Section 9: Forfeited Game Records

If a game is forfeited, no individual records are kept unless the game was regulation at the time.

### Section 10: Scoring (Tie-Breaker)

In scoring a tie-breaker game, a run scored by a player who started the inning at second base (under the tie-breaker rule) is charged to the defensive team, not to the pitcher.

## Rule 12: Use of Rules

*(Code of Conduct and Ethics)*

The spirit and intent of all USA Softball rules is to encourage sportsmanship and fair play. Rules should be applied consistently and in accordance with their intent. Any violation of the rules that is not specifically addressed by an explicit penalty should be handled in a manner consistent with the overall intent of the rules.
