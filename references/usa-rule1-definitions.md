# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 1: Definitions

*These definitions apply to all USA Softball rules. Fast Pitch (FP) and Junior Olympic (JO) specific notes are included where applicable.*

---

**Altered Bat** — A bat that has been changed from its original manufactured condition. Any bat that is determined to be altered shall be removed from the game.

**Appeal Play** — A play on which an umpire cannot make a ruling until a manager or player requests that the umpire make a call. An appeal must be made before the next legal or illegal pitch, or before all fielders and the pitcher leave fair territory.

**At Bat** — The period during which a player is at bat. A batter is at bat until they are put out, reach base safely, or the inning ends.

**Ball** — An illegal pitch or a pitched ball that does not enter the strike zone.

**Base** — One of four points which must be touched by the runner in order to score a run. Bases are 15 inches square; first base uses a double base (white and colored portions).

**Base Line / Base Path** — A direct line between bases. A runner is entitled to a path up to 3 feet to either side of a direct line between bases when being tagged.

**Base Hit** — A hit by which the batter safely reaches base without an error or fielder's choice.

**Base on Balls (Walk)** — An award of first base to a batter who receives four balls during a time at bat. In Fast Pitch, four pitches must be thrown.

**Batter** — An offensive player who takes their position in the batter's box.

**Batter-Runner** — A term that describes the offensive player who has just finished their turn at bat and has not yet been put out or reached first base.

**Battery** — The pitcher and the catcher.

**Blocked Ball** — A batted or thrown ball that is touched, stopped, or handled by a person not engaged in the game, or a thrown ball that touches an offensive or defensive team member's equipment or other material left outside the dugout or bench.

**Bunt** — A batted ball not swung at but intentionally met with the bat and tapped slowly within the infield. In Slow Pitch, a bunt is an illegal hit.

**Called Game** — A game terminated by the umpire before it becomes a regulation game, usually due to weather, darkness, or other conditions.

**Catch** — The act of a fielder in getting secure possession in the hand or glove of a ball in flight and firmly holding it, providing the catch is made before the ball touches the ground. Bobbling or dropping the ball after securing it is not a catch.

**Catcher's Obstruction** — The interference of the catcher with the batter's attempt to hit a pitched ball. See Rule 8, Section 1, Article D.

**Chopped Ball** — A ball which is struck with a downward blow, causing it to bounce high in the air.

**Coaching Box** — A box marked on each side of home plate, to which coaches must confine themselves.

**Commitment Line** — (Fast Pitch) A line drawn through fair territory, 8 feet from home plate, extending from the back corner of home plate to the edge of the playing field in foul territory. Used to determine when a runner must commit to home plate or be put out between the commitment line and home plate. A runner who crosses the commitment line must continue to home plate (or the scoring line).

**Courtesy Runner** — A player who may run for the pitcher or catcher at any time without being charged as a substitute. See Rule 8, Section 9.

**Crow Hop** — (Fast Pitch) A replant of the pivot foot prior to delivering the pitch. This can occur by: (1) sliding the foot in a direction not in contact with the pitcher's plate; (2) lifting the pivot foot and stepping forward; or (3) jumping forward with the pivot foot with the pivot foot prior to starting the pitch. A crow hop is an illegal pitch.

**Dead Ball** — A ball out of play because of a legally created suspension of play. No action by either team counts during a dead ball.

**Defensive Team** — The team in the field.

**Designated Player (DP)** — (Fast Pitch) A player who bats for one of the nine starting defensive players. The DP may also play defense for the FLEX player. See Rules Supplement #15.

**Double Base (Safety Base)** — First base is a double base, consisting of a white (fair territory) portion and a colored (foul territory) portion, each 15" x 15". The batter-runner uses the colored portion on an overthrow play.

**Dropped Third Strike** — (Fast Pitch) If the catcher fails to catch a pitch that the umpire has called strike three, the batter-runner may attempt to advance to first base. This is only permitted when: first base is unoccupied at the time of the pitch, OR there are two outs. The batter is not out until tagged or thrown out at first base.

**Fair Ball** — A batted ball that settles on fair territory between home and first base or between home and third base; or that bounds past first or third base on or over fair territory; or that first falls on fair territory beyond first or third base; or touches first, second, or third base.

**Fair Territory** — The playing field within and including the first and third base foul lines, from home plate to the bottom of the playing field fence.

**Fake Tag** — When a fielder without the ball obstructs a runner by simulating a tag. Fake tags are obstruction and must be called. See Rules Supplement #19.

**Fielder** — Any one of the nine players of the defensive team.

**Fielder's Choice** — The act of a fielder who handles a fair grounder and, instead of throwing to first base to retire the batter-runner, throws to another base in an attempt to retire a preceding runner.

**FLEX Player** — (Fast Pitch) A player who plays defense in the 10th position for the Designated Player (DP). The FLEX player does not bat.

**Fly Ball** — A batted ball that goes into the air.

**Force Out** — A putout of a runner who is compelled to advance because the batter becomes a batter-runner and the runner has not reached the next base before a fielder has the ball touching that base. A runner can be forced out only while in the act of advancing. See Rules Supplement #21.

**Forfeited Game** — A game declared ended by the umpire in favor of the offended team.

**Foul Ball** — A batted ball that settles on foul territory between home and first base, or between home and third base; or that bounds past first or third base on or over foul territory; or that first falls on foul territory beyond first or third base; or that touches the person of an umpire or player on foul territory.

**Foul Tip** — A batted ball that goes sharply and directly from the bat to the catcher's hands or glove and is legally caught. A foul tip is a strike. The ball is in play. On a third strike, if the catcher catches the foul tip, the batter is out.

**Ground Ball** — A batted ball that rolls or bounces along the ground.

**Home Team** — The team on whose field the game is played. In a neutral site game, home team is designated by the coin flip or standings.

**Illegal Bat** — A bat that does not meet specifications in Rule 3, Section 1.

**Illegal Pitch** — (Fast Pitch) A pitch not delivered in compliance with Rule 6A. Results in a ball being awarded to the batter.

**Infield Fly** — A fair fly ball (not a line drive or a bunt) which can be caught by an infielder with ordinary effort, when first and second bases, or first, second, and third bases are occupied before two are out. The batter is automatically out when the infield fly is declared by the umpire. Runners may advance at their own risk.

**In Flight** — A batted, thrown, or pitched ball that has not yet touched the ground or any object other than a fielder.

**In Jeopardy** — A term indicating that the ball is in play and an offensive player may be put out.

**Intentional Walk** — (Fast Pitch) When the defensive team elects to walk the batter intentionally. Four pitches must be thrown to the catcher.

**Interference** — An act of an offensive player or team member that impedes, hinders, or confuses a defensive player attempting to execute a play. Also: the act of a fielder not entitled to field a ball who obstructs a runner. See Rules Supplement #33.

**Inning** — That portion of a game within which the teams alternate on offense and defense, each team having three outs. A half-inning begins when a team takes the field and ends when the third out is made.

**Legal Delivery (Legal Pitch)** — In Fast Pitch, a pitch delivered in accordance with Rule 6A, Section 3.

**Line Drive** — A batted ball that travels in a relatively straight line through or close to the infield.

**Look Back Rule** — (Fast Pitch / Modified Pitch) Once the pitcher has possession of the ball within the 8-foot circle and the batter-runner has touched first base and the play is over, all runners must immediately advance to the next base or return to the last base occupied. If the runner stops or reverses direction, the runner is out. See Rules Supplement #34.

**Obstruction** — The act of a fielder not in possession of the ball and not in the act of fielding a batted ball that impedes the progress of a batter-runner or runner who is legally running the bases. See Rules Supplement #36.

**Offensive Team** — The team at bat.

**Overslide** — The act of a base runner who, in attempting to reach a base, slides beyond the base and loses contact with it.

**Passed Ball** — A pitched ball that the catcher fails to stop or control with ordinary effort, allowing a runner to advance.

**Pitcher's Plate** — The rubber plate (24" x 6") from which the pitcher must deliver the ball.

**Pivot Foot** — (Fast Pitch) The foot that the pitcher must keep in contact with the pitcher's plate until the ball is released. For right-handed pitchers, this is the right foot; for left-handed pitchers, it is the left foot.

**Play Ball** — The umpire's command for the game or play to start or resume.

**Quick Return Pitch** — A pitch made with the obvious intent to catch the batter off balance. This is an illegal pitch. See Rule 6A, Section 10.

**Regulation Game** — A game that has progressed to the point where a winner can be determined (generally 5 complete innings, or 4½ innings with the home team ahead).

**Runner** — An offensive player who has legally reached a base and has not yet been put out.

**Sacrifice Fly** — A batted fly ball that is caught by an outfielder and allows a runner to tag up and score, provided there are fewer than two outs at the time of the hit.

**Short-Handed Rule** — The rule allowing a team to play with one less than the required number of players. See Rules Supplement #47–48.

**Steal** — The advance of a base runner without the benefit of a hit, walk, error, or passed ball. In Fast Pitch, runners may steal at any time after the pitcher releases the ball.

**Strike Zone** — (Fast Pitch) The space over home plate that is between the batter's armpits and the top of the knees when the batter assumes a natural batting stance.

**Three-Foot Running Lane** — A lane extending from home plate to first base (the last half of the baseline). The batter-runner must run within this lane when a play is being made at first base with a thrown ball. The lines of the lane are considered part of the lane. See Rule 8, Section 2, Article E.

**Tie Game** — A regulation game that has been called when both teams have scored an equal number of runs.

**Wild Pitch** — A pitch too high, too low, or too wide for the catcher to control with ordinary effort.
