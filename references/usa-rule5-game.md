# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 5: The Game

---

## Section 1: Starting the Game

**A.** The home team takes the field first. The visiting team bats first (top half of the inning).

**B.** The managers must exchange lineup cards and review ground rules with the umpire at the Pre-Game Meeting (plate conference). Once the manager approves and returns the lineup card to the umpire, any changes are considered substitutions.

**C.** The batting order becomes official when the plate umpire receives and approves it.

---

## Section 2: Fitness of the Grounds

The umpire shall determine if the field is fit for play and shall have the authority to delay or cancel a game due to unsafe conditions.

---

## Section 3: Regulation Game / Duration

**A.** A regulation game consists of **7 innings** (each team bats once per inning = 14 half-innings).

**B.** A game is considered regulation (official) if:
- 5 or more complete innings have been played (or 4½ innings with the home team ahead), or
- The game is called due to weather, darkness, or other conditions after becoming official

**C.** If a game is called before it becomes regulation, it shall be rescheduled as a new game (unless local rules specify differently).

**D.** A called game: If a regulation game is called, the score at the end of the last complete inning shall stand, unless the home team has tied or taken the lead in their incomplete inning. In that case, the score shall be the score at the time the game was called.

**E.** Tied regulation games go into extra innings.

**F.** Run-ahead (mercy) rule: If one team is sufficiently far ahead, the game may be ended early. USA Softball does not specify a standard mercy rule for JO Girls Fast Pitch — mercy rules vary by association and are set locally.

---

## Section 4: Forfeited Games

**A.** A game shall be forfeited to the opponent when a team:
1. Fails to appear on the field or refuses to continue playing
2. Refuses to start play within a reasonable time after the umpire calls "Play Ball"
3. Has fewer than the minimum number of players required to play
4. Employs tactics to delay or shorten the game
5. Willfully violates rules after being warned by the umpire

**B.** Score of a forfeited game: 7-0 (or league-specified score).

---

## Section 5: Scoring a Run

**A.** A run is scored when a runner legally touches home plate before the third out of the inning, provided the third out is not:
1. A force out (including batter-runner being put out at first base before reaching the base), or
2. A runner called out for passing a preceding runner

**B.** If the third out results from a batter-runner being put out before reaching first base, or from a force play, no run scores regardless of runners already crossing home plate.

**C.** See Rules Supplement #43 for run scoring on the third out of an inning.

---

## Section 6: Incomplete Innings

If a game is called while an inning is incomplete:
- If the visiting team scores to take the lead or tie it in the top of the inning, and the home team hasn't batted, the game reverts to the last completed inning
- If the home team has taken the lead at the time the game is called, the current score stands

---

## Section 7: Defensive Conferences

*(Also see Rules Supplement #9)*

**A. Defensive charged conferences:** A defensive charged conference occurs when the defensive team requests a suspension of play for any reason, and a representative enters the playing field to communicate with any defensive player.
- A team is allowed **three (3) charged conferences per seven innings** (plus one more per extra inning).
- If the pitcher is removed for a conference, it is not a charged conference (since the pitcher is already being replaced).
- **On the second conference in an extra inning**, the pitcher must be removed.
- In extra inning games, there will be one charged conference allowed per extra inning.

**B. Offensive charged conferences:** One (1) offensive charged conference allowed per inning. The umpires should refuse to grant the second conference.

**C.** A conference should not be charged when the umpire calls time for a legitimate reason (injury, blood rule, field repair, scorekeeper issue, etc.).

## Section 9: Short-Handed Rule

*(See Rules Supplement #47–48)*

**A.** All divisions may begin a game one player short: Slow Pitch may start with 9 players; Fast Pitch may start with 8 players. When playing short, an out must be recorded when the absent player's batting position comes up.

**B.** In Fast Pitch, a team must have at least 8 players to begin. If a team begins with 8 players, an out is recorded in the 9th batting position each time it comes up.

**C.** After starting with a full team, a team may continue with one fewer player if a player leaves for any reason other than ejection (short-handed rule). If a player leaves due to ejection, an out is recorded each time the ejected player's batting position comes up.

---

## Section 10: Time Limit

Time limit rules vary by association and division. Check your league's local rules for specific time limits.

---

## Section 11: Tie-Breaker Rule (Girls JO Fast Pitch)

*(See Rules Supplement #54)*

In Girls JO Fast Pitch, the tie-breaker places the **last scheduled batter** from the prior inning at second base with no outs at the start of each extra inning. A substitute may be inserted for the runner. The run scored by a player starting as a runner at second base is charged to the defensive team (not the pitcher's ERA).


---

## Section 12: Dugout Conduct

*(See Rules Supplement #16)*

Once the game begins, only players involved in the game may be outside the dugout (exceptions: coaching boxes, on-deck circle, field when called by umpire). Players may not use tobacco or alcohol in the dugout or on the playing field.
