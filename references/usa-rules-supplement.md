# USA Softball 2026 — Rules Supplement (RS)

*Numbered supplemental rules providing additional guidance. All references are to Fast Pitch / JO Girls 12U unless noted.*

---

## RS #1 — Appeals

**Types of appealable plays:**
1. Missing a base (forward or backward) — but NOT touching only the white portion of the double base when a play is made at first (this IS an error; also not applicable on errant throw)
2. Leaving a base too soon on a caught fly ball
3. Attempting to advance to second base after making a turn at first base
4. Batting out of order

**Live ball appeals (A):** During a live ball, any fielder in possession of the ball may verbally appeal or touch the base where the infraction occurred. The umpire then rules.

**Dead ball appeals (B):** Once all runners complete their advancement:
- Any coach or infielder may verbally make an appeal
- The pitcher and catcher are considered infielders for appeals
- Verbal appeal alone (with the ball in possession) is sufficient

**When appeals must be made (E):**
- Before the next pitch (legal or illegal)
- At the end of an inning, before the pitcher and all infielders leave fair territory

**May Not Return (D):** A runner may not return to touch a missed base or one left too soon on a caught fly ball if:
1. They have left the field of play
2. A following runner has scored
3. Once a runner advances to and passes the next base awarded

**Force Out on Appeal (J):** When an appeal is the third out of an inning and a force is involved, no runs score. If the force out is not involved (it is a time play), any runs scored before the appeal are counted.

**Tag-Ups (K):** When a runner leaves a base too soon on a caught fly ball and returns to attempt to retouch, this is considered a time play, not a force out. All runs scored advance of the appealed runner and prior to the legal appeal count.

**Missing First Base Before Throw Arrives (L):** When a runner passes first base before a throw arrives, they are considered to have touched the base unless properly appealed.

**Fourth-Out Appeal (M):** An appeal may be made after the third out of an inning as long as it is made properly. The appeal must be made on a runner who has scored but missed a base or left too soon. EXAMPLE: One out, runners on 1st and 3rd. Batter hits a fly ball that is caught (2nd out). Both runners leave their bases before the catch. An appeal is made at 1st base (3rd out). The defense makes an appeal at 3rd base (potential 4th out). If the 3rd base runner left too soon and this is the fourth out, the run at home does not count.

**End of Game (N):** On the last play of the game, appeals may be made until the umpires leave the field. Once umpires leave, no appeal is possible.

## RS #2 — Ball Rotation Procedure

Many associations use a ball rotation procedure:
- The pitcher has a choice of which ball to use at the start of each half-inning
- If both balls don't get into play in the first half of the first inning, the pitcher MUST throw the unused ball
- The current ball should be used until it goes out of play or becomes unplayable
- If the pitcher requests another ball because a ball is unplayable, give them another ball and remove the unplayable one. Do NOT give the pitcher a choice of balls.
- After an inning, return the ball to the vicinity of the pitcher's plate. The pitcher choosing the field now has a ball and may request another from the plate umpire.

---

## RS #3 — Bat with Dents, Rattles, Excessive Wear / Delaminations

Bats with dents, excessive wear, or rattles shall be removed and held until the championship is over. When noticeable delamination appears on an Easton-style bat on the barrel area more than an inch in any direction, remove the bat and hold it until the end of the championship.

---

## RS #4 — Batting with Illegal, Altered, or Non-Approved Bat

When a batter uses an illegal/altered/non-approved bat and reaches base safely, and the next batter steps into the box with the same bat:
- **A.** If noticed before a pitch to the next batter, the umpire calls out the batter who used the bat.
- **B.** If the bat is altered or non-approved, the player is ejected and subject to further penalties. The bat is removed. Runners put out prior to discovering this remain out; runners not put out return to the base they occupied at the time of the batted ball.
- **C.** After a pitch to the current batter, that batter is called out. No penalty to the previous batter.

---

## RS #5 — Batter Remains in Batter's Box (JO Fast Pitch)

This rule is not intended to penalize the player; it is used to speed up the game. The batter may still receive a signal with one foot in the box and take practice swings. If the batter leaves the box illegally, a warning should be given. A strike should not be called without a warning. One, two, or three strikes may be called on the same batter under this ruling.

---

## RS #6 — Batting Order Is Official

The batting order is official when reviewed by the manager and the plate umpire at the Pre-Game Meeting. Once the manager approves the lineup and returns it to the umpire, any changes thereafter are considered substitutions.

---

## RS #7 — Batting Out of the Batter's Box

To call a batter out for batting out of the batter's box, one foot or both feet must be completely outside the lines of the batter's box when contact is made with the ball. The lines of the batter's box are considered inside the box. Good judgment should be used, and the benefit of the doubt goes to the batter.

A batter should be called out immediately for hitting a pitched ball while out of the batter's box. The ball is dead and the batter is out whether the ball is fair or foul. When there are no batter's box lines, good judgment should be used.

---

## RS #8 — Catcher's Box

The catcher's box is described in Rule 2, Section 3D.

**(Fast Pitch)** Catchers must remain in the catcher's box until the pitch is released. The catcher may move closer to the plate without penalty when the batter is positioned in the front portion of the batter's box during a pitch. However, the catcher must at all times avoid catcher's obstruction, as the batter has the right to the entire batter's box. Obstruction does not require contact between the catcher and the bat or the batter. The umpire's request for the catcher to move farther away to avoid injury or obstruction must be obeyed. (See catcher obstruction under RS #36.)

**(Slow Pitch)** The catcher may not have any part of their body or equipment touching the ground outside the lines of the catcher's box until the ball is batted, touches the ground or plate, hits the batter, or reaches the catcher's box. It is an illegal pitch if the catcher touches outside the catcher's box. The catcher, even when within the catcher's box, may still obstruct the batter.

---

## RS #9 — Charged Conferences

**A. Defensive charged conference:** When the defensive team requests suspension of play and a representative enters the field to communicate with any defensive player.
- A team is allowed **3 defensive charged conferences per 7 innings** (1 additional per extra inning).
- If the pitcher is removed for a conference, it is NOT a charged conference.
- **On the 2nd conference in an extra inning, the pitcher must be removed.**
- If the pitcher returns to pitching after being removed and a pitch is thrown, the pitcher is disqualified.

**B. Offensive charged conference:** Only **one (1) offensive charged conference per inning** is allowed. Umpires should refuse to grant a second conference. If the offense insists on a second conference, the umpire should eject the team representative.

It is NOT an offensive conference when a representative confers with a batter and/or runner during a defensive charged conference (as long as both teams are ready to play when the defense is ready, or when the pitcher is putting on a warm-up jacket).

---

## RS #10 — Check Swing / Bunt Strike

Four areas determine a swing:
- Did the batter roll their wrists?
- Did the batter swing through the ball and bring the bat back, unless the batter draws the bat back before the pitch arrives?
- Was the bat out in front of the body?
- Did the batter make an attempt to hit the pitch?

On a bunt attempt: was the bat moved toward the ball? The umpire should consider: Was the pitch in the strike zone? Did the batter swing at the pitched ball, or (on a bunt attempt) did the batter move the bat toward the ball?

If the umpire calls the pitch a ball and the catcher requests a check swing ruling, the plate umpire should ask the base umpire ONLY if there is doubt. If the base umpire is asked and calls a strike, or if the ball was a bunt attempt that is fouled off on the 3rd strike, the dropped 3rd strike rule applies.

---

## RS #11 — Communication / Electronic Devices

**A.** Communication/electronic devices, including audio/video equipment, used to communicate between coaches or with anyone outside the field of play are not allowed on the playing field. Head phones and ear phones used to communicate between coaches' boxes, between coaches and the dugout, or from the stands to the dugout are prohibited. Umpires must prohibit any use of such equipment. Electronic scorebooks are permitted for use by base coaches.

**B.** An exclusively one-way electronic communication device may be used by the defense or offense to relay the pitch or play call from the dugout or field.

---

## RS #12 — Courtesy Runner

**Fast Pitch / Modified Pitch:**
- Only the **pitcher and catcher** may use a courtesy runner
- The courtesy runner must be a player **not in the lineup** (not currently in the batting order). For JO Pool Play Only, any player may be a courtesy runner.
- A player may be a courtesy runner **only once per inning**
- No charged substitution
- An unreported courtesy runner is disqualified upon the offended team's appeal

**Senior Men's and Senior Women's Slow Pitch:** The courtesy runner may be ANY player on the line-up card (once per inning for anyone other than pitcher/catcher).

---

## RS #13 — Crashing Into a Fielder With the Ball (Interference)

To protect a defensive player holding the ball and waiting to apply a tag, a runner must be called out when they crash into the fielder. The runner may slide, jump over, go around, or return to the previous base. If the crash is flagrant, the offender is ejected.

**Effects:**
- **A.** Ball is dead; runner is out. Each runner must return to the last base touched at time of the crash (this constitutes interference).
- **B.** If the crash was intended to break up an obvious double play, the immediate succeeding runner is also out.
- **C.** When the crash occurs after the runner is called out, the runner closest to home plate is also called out.
- **D.** If an obstructed runner crashes into a fielder, the obstruction is ignored and the runner is out.
- **E.** When a runner runs outside the three-foot running lane to avoid a crash with a fielder, the runner should be called out.
- **F.** When a defensive player is fielding a thrown ball and the flight carries them into the runner's path, it is not a crash.

---

## RS #14 — Delayed Dead Ball

There are five situations where a rule violation occurs but the ball remains live until the conclusion of play:
1. **An illegal pitch** (Rule 6A — Fast Pitch)
2. **Catcher's obstruction** (Rule 8, Section 1D)
3. **Plate umpire interference** (Rule 8, Section 6F)
4. **Obstruction** (Rule 8, Section 5B)
5. **Batted or thrown ball contacts detached equipment** (Rule 8, Section 5F)

In each case, the umpire signals a delayed dead ball, the play runs its course, and then the appropriate penalty is applied.

---

## RS #15 — Designated Player (DP) (Fast Pitch)

The DP may be listed in any of the nine batting positions. The player listed 10th in the batting order plays defense for the DP and is called the **FLEX**.

- **A.** A DP may be used for any defensive player, made known to the plate umpire prior to game start. The DP must be on the line-up card as one of the 9 hitters.
- **B.** The FLEX must be placed in the 10th position in the line-up card.
- **C.** The DP and FLEX must remain in the same positions in the batting order throughout the game.
- **D.** The DP may be substituted for by a pinch hitter or runner. The substitute becomes the DP.
- **E.** The FLEX may be substituted at any time. The substitute becomes the FLEX.
- **F.** The DP may be replaced by the FLEX as a hitter or runner. This reduces the number of players from 10 to 9.
- **G.** The FLEX may be replaced by the DP on defense. This reduces the number from 10 to 9.
- **H.** The starting DP may re-enter the game once, only in the original DP position.
- **I.** The starting FLEX may re-enter the game once, in the original FLEX position or in the DP batting position.
- **J.** The DP may play defense for any player (other than the FLEX) without removing that player from the game.
- **K.** The role of the DP/FLEX is never terminated. The team may go from 10 to 9 players and back as many times during the game as desired.
- **L.** If the DP replaces the FLEX or the FLEX replaces the DP, the change must be reported to the umpire.

---

## RS #16 — Dugout Conduct

Once the game begins, only players involved in the game may be outside the dugout, except where the rules allow or an umpire grants permission. No one may smoke or use alcohol in the dugout or on the playing field. On-deck batters must be in the on-deck circle. Players may not stand near the backstop to observe the pitcher's delivery.

---

## RS #17 — Equipment on the Playing Field

Loose equipment, miscellaneous items, or detached parts of a player's uniform (other than those being legally used in the game) should not be within live ball territory. Official equipment that may be in live ball territory with no penalty includes: the batter's bat, the catcher's mask, umpire paraphernalia, a helmet that has inadvertently fallen off a player during play, or any equipment belonging to a person assigned to the game.

**A. Thrown Ball:**
1. When a thrown ball hits loose equipment belonging to the team at bat, the ball is dead immediately. If such action interferes with a play, interference is ruled — the runner being played on is called out and each runner returns to the last base touched prior to the interference. If no play was apparent, a blocked ball is ruled; no one is called out and all runners return to the last base touched.
2. When the loose equipment belongs to the team in the field, it becomes a blocked ball and the overthrow rule applies (two-base award).

**B. Batted Ball:**
1. A batted foul ball touching loose equipment is a foul ball.
2. A batted fair ball touching loose equipment belonging to:
   - **a.** The offense: it is a dead ball and runners return, unless they are forced to advance when the batter-runner is awarded first base on a base hit.
   - **b.** The defense: it is a dead ball and all runners, including the batter-runner, are awarded two bases from their position at the time of the pitch.

---

## RS #18 — Extra Player (EP) — Slow Pitch / JO Fast Pitch

**Slow Pitch:** If a team uses the EP, it must be indicated on the line-up card at the start of the game. The team must end the game with 11 players or play using the shorthanded rule. All 11 players bat, but only 10 play defense. Defensive changes may be made at any time; however, the batting order may not change. Any of the 11 starting players may leave the game once and re-enter. A starting player and their substitute may not be in the game at the same time.

**JO Fast Pitch:** Any batters in addition to the defensive players and/or DP/FLEX shall be listed as EPs. Defensive changes may be made at any time; however, the batting order may not change.
- **Pool Play Only:** Multiple EPs may be used; they must be indicated on the line-up card. If an injury occurs, the line-up is compressed with no penalty. Teams may not continue with fewer than eight players.
- **All Other Play:** If a team uses the EP, it must be indicated on the line-up card at the start of the game. The team must end the game with 10 players batting or play using the shorthanded rule.

---

## RS #19 — Fake Tag

A fake tag occurs when a fielder without the ball deceives the runner by simulating a tag, causing the runner to slide, slow down, or stop.

- **A.** Obstruction is called when a fake tag is made. Umpire signals delayed dead ball and lets play continue to its completion.
- **B.** The umpire should rule obstruction on all fake tags. Continued fake tags should result in ejections.
- **C.** When a fielder fakes a tag but the runner continues to the next base without sliding or breaking stride, there is no rule violation (though a warning should be given).

---

## RS #20 — Falling Over the Fence on a Catch

The fence is an extension of the playing field. When a player catches a ball in the air and their momentum carries them over or through the fence, the catch is legal — the batter-runner is out. The ball is dead. Runners advance one base without liability to be put out.

- **A.** If the player catches the ball before touching the ground outside the playing area, it is a legal catch.
- **B.** If the player catches the ball after touching the ground outside the playing area, it is not a catch.

A portable, collapsible fence that is not anchored may be climbed; if the defensive player is standing on the fence when the catch is made, it is legal.

---

## RS #21 — Force Out

A force out can be made by tagging a runner who is forced to advance to a base, or by touching the base to which the runner is forced. It is not possible to have a force out on a caught fly ball (the batter-runner is no longer a runner). On an appeal play, the force out is determined when the appeal is made, not when the infraction occurred.

---

## RS #22 — Foul Ball / Foul Tip

The "height of the batter's head" criterion no longer applies. Umpires only judge whether the ball moves sharply and directly from the bat to the catcher's glove/mitt. A foul tip is caught by the catcher when:
- **A.** A foul ball with a "perceptible arc" goes toward the catcher's left, and the catcher moves their glove to catch it — batter is out, ball remains live.
- **B.** The foul ball rebounds from the bat with a "perceptible arc" shoulder-high, and the catcher moves upward to catch it — batter is out, ball remains live.
- **C.** The pitcher throws a rise ball and the catcher is moving up as the pitch is released; the batter squares to bunt and the ball goes in a straight line from the bat to the glove — this is a foul tip. It is a foul tip because the ball goes directly from bat to the catcher's glove/mitt regardless of height.

Umpires must be alert to tag-up opportunities on batted foul fly balls.

---

## RS #23 — Glove/Mitt (Using an Illegal Glove/Mitt)

If discovered, it should be corrected immediately. If a player refuses to correct the use of an illegal glove/mitt, they may not play.

---

## RS #24 — Hitting the Ball a Second Time

When a batter hits the pitched ball a second time, the umpire places the act into one of three categories:
- **A.** If the bat is in the batter's hands and the ball comes into contact with the bat, and the batter is in the batter's box, it is a foul ball.
- **B.** If the bat is out of the batter's hands, dropped, or thrown, and hits the ball in fair territory, the ball is dead and the batter-runner is out.
- **C.** If a batter swings and misses and then accidentally hits the ball on the follow-through, intentionally hits it on the second swing, or hits the ball after it bounces off the catcher or mitt or glove, the ball is dead and all runners return.

*(FP with stealing — on 2 and 3, if the act is intentional with runners on base, batter is out for interference and runners return.)*

---

## RS #25 — Home Runs (Slow Pitch) and Running Bases

*Applies only to all Adult and Junior Olympic Boys Slow Pitch.* When an over-the-fence home run is hit, the batter and all base runners may go directly to the dugout. Appeals for runners missing a base are not allowed. The batter-runner or runners may not be called out for any base running infraction. This speeds up the game as batters and runners do not have to run the bases.

---

## RS #26 — Home Runs / Four-Base Awards / Two-Base Awards

- A batted ball that does not hit the ground and leaves the playing field in fair territory is a home run.
- **A.** A batted ball hitting a foul pole above the fence is a home run.
- **B.** A batted ball hitting the top of the fence and bouncing over in fair territory is a home run.
- **C.** A batted ball hitting a defensive player in fair territory but going over the fence in foul territory is a two-base award.
- **D.** A batted ball that leaves the park in fair territory but first touches the ground in foul territory is a home run.
- **E.** A batted ball that touches the fence, then touches a player, and goes over the fence in fair territory is a two-base award.

---

## RS #27 — Imaginary Line or Dead Ball Area

When a fielder carries a live ball into a dead ball area, the ball becomes dead and bases are awarded. If the act is **unintentional**, the award is one base. If **intentional**, the award is two bases. The base award is governed from the last base legally touched at the time the ball became dead.

When a chalk line is used to determine an out-of-play area, the line is in play. A fielder touching the line is in the field of play and may make a legal catch or throw. If a foot is on the ground completely in dead ball territory (not touching the line), the ball is dead and no play may be made. If a fielder catches the ball with one foot inside the line or touching the line and another foot in the air, the catch is legal; if the fielder then steps completely into dead ball territory, the ball becomes dead and all runners are awarded one base from the last base touched.

Defensive players cannot intentionally throw or step into a dead ball area to prevent a runner from returning to a missed base. If so ruled, allow the runner to return to the base.

---

## RS #28 — Infielder

An infielder, pitcher, or catcher is any player who defends the area around first, second, third base, or shortstop areas. This definition is used for the infield fly rule and for determining if a runner hit by a batted ball is out.

---

## RS #29 — Injured Player

During any live ball, when a player becomes injured and the umpire determines it requires immediate attention, the umpire should call time out and allow first aid. Runners on base should be awarded the bases the umpire judges they would have reached if time had not been called.

---

## RS #30 — Intentionally Dropped Fly Ball

The ball cannot be intentionally dropped unless the fielder has actually caught it and then drops it. Merely guiding the ball to the ground is not an intentionally dropped ball. An intentionally dropped ball is treated the same as a caught fly ball — the batter is out and runners are not forced to advance.

---

## RS #31 — Intentional Walk (Fast Pitch)

The ball is live during an intentional walk in fast pitch. All defensive players must be in fair territory until the pitch is released, except the catcher (who must be in the catcher's box at the start of each pitch). All four pitches must be thrown to the catcher.

---

## RS #32 — Intentional Walk (Slow Pitch)

An intentional walk in Slow Pitch occurs during a dead ball — no pitch is required. The defensive team notifies the plate umpire of their intent; the umpire calls time out and awards the batter first base without liability to be put out. A possible appeal on a previous play is nullified. When two successive batters are walked intentionally, the second walk may not be administered until the first batter reaches first base.

---

## RS #33 — Interference

Interference is the act of an offensive player or team member that impedes, hinders, or confuses a defensive player attempting to execute a play.

**A. Runner interference includes:**
1. A runner or batter-runner who interferes with a fielder executing a play, including touching the white portion of the double base when a play is made at first (when they should use the orange portion), and colliding with the fielder from a thrown ball.

*(A) When a runner interferes with a fielder, the umpire must determine if the interference occurred before or after the runner who interfered was put out. Merely running in front of a batted ball or jumping over a batted ball is not interference. For a runner to be called out, they must commit an act of interference.*

*(B) When a runner is hit by a fair batted ball, it is interference if it occurred before the ball passed an infielder (excluding the pitcher) and the runner was not in contact with the base.*

*(C) A runner could be standing on a base and a defensive player bumps the runner while watching the flight of the ball. If the defensive player fails to make a catch on a ball that could have been caught, it is the umpire's judgment whether or not interference should be called.*

*(D) If interference occurs by the runner on a foul fly ball not caught, in the umpire's judgment could have been caught with ordinary effort, the runner is out and the batter is also out. If, in the umpire's judgment, the foul fly ball could not have been caught with ordinary effort, the call is dead, and the batter remains at bat.*

*(E) For crash interference, refer to RS #13.*

2. A runner or batter-runner who is hit by a fair touched or untouched batted ball.
3. Interfering with a thrown ball.

**B. Batter interference** occurs while the batter is at bat and before the ball is batted. It occurs in fast pitch when the batter interferes with the catcher's throw on an attempted steal or an attempted pick-off from the catcher. Interference could also occur when a batter releases the bat in a manner that hits the catcher and prevents them from making a play.

**C. Offensive players in the dugout** may be charged with interference if they interfere with a fielder's opportunity to make an out on a fly ball.

**D. On-deck batters** may be charged with interference if they interfere with a throw, a possible tag on a runner, or a fielder's opportunity to make an out on a fly ball.

**E. Coach's interference** occurs when a base coach runs toward home and draws a throw or when they interfere with a fielder attempting to catch or throw a ball.

**F. Spectator interference** occurs when a spectator:
1. Enters the field and interferes with a play — *Effect: The batter and runner should be placed at the bases that the umpire judges they would have reached had the interference not occurred. The ball belongs to the fielder and the stands belong to the spectator.*
2. Reaches onto the field from the stands and prevents a fielder from catching a fly ball — *Effect: A dead ball is ruled and the batter is out. All other runners should be placed at the bases that the umpire judges they would have reached had the interference not occurred.*

**G. Umpire interference** occurs when:
1. An umpire is hit by a fair, untouched batted ball before it passes an infielder, excluding the pitcher. The batter-runner is awarded first base.
2. (Fast Pitch & Slow Pitch with Stealing) When an umpire interferes with a catcher's attempt to put out a runner stealing or an attempted pick-off from the catcher. It is interference only if the runner is not put out. If the runner is not put out, runners return to the base they occupied at the time of the pitch.

**H. Offensive team interference** occurs when a thrown ball strikes loose equipment left in live ball territory.

**I. Batter-runner interference** occurs when the batter-runner steps back toward home plate to avoid a tag by a defensive player.

## RS #34 — Look Back Rule (Fast Pitch / Modified Pitch)

*(See also Rule 8, Section 10 for detailed rules)*

When a runner is legitimately off base after a pitch, or as a result of the batter completing their turn at bat, and is stationary when the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the runner MUST immediately attempt to advance to the next base or immediately return to the base last occupied.

**A.** The Look Back rule does not go into effect for any runner until the batter-runner touches first base or is called out, and the pitcher has control of the ball within the eight-foot radius circle.

**B.** Failure to immediately proceed to the next base or return to their original base after the pitcher has the ball in the circle results in the runner being called out.

**C. Exception B-C:** A runner will not be declared out when:
1. A play is made on another runner
2. The pitcher leaves the circle or drops the ball
3. The pitcher releases the ball to the batter
4. The pitcher places the ball under their arm or between their legs to free both hands (ball not considered "controlled")

**D.** A ball on balls or dropped third strike: Runners may advance until the batter-runner reaches first base. Once the batter-runner touches first base and the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the Look Back rule applies.

**E.** A pitcher fielding a ball in the circle: A pitcher fielding a ball in the circle is an infielder. Runners may leave their base.

**F.** Multiple runners off base: If the ball is dead and it is impossible to call two outs on the Look Back rule, the ball is dead. The first runner declared out remains out; all other runners return to the last base they left.

**G-H.** Batter-runner overrunning first base: If the batter-runner stops while overrunning first base and the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the Look Back rule applies. If the batter-runner stops at first base, they are safe. If they stop between first and second, and the pitcher has the ball in the circle, the Look Back rule applies.

**L.** Being in the circle: The pitcher is considered in the circle if both feet are within or partially within the lines. If either foot is completely outside the lines, the pitcher is not considered in the circle.

---

## RS #35 — Media Coverage

Authorized media may be on the playing field. All media must be able to move to avoid being hit by an errant throw or batted ball. Photographic equipment must be carried, not left on the ground. If an errant throw or batted ball becomes lodged in the photographer's equipment, the overthrow or out-of-play rules apply.

---

## RS #36 — Obstruction

Obstruction is the act of a fielder:
- **A.** Not in possession of the ball, or
- **B.** Not in the act of fielding a batted ball, that impedes the progress of a batter-runner or runner who is legally running the bases.

It is obstruction if a defensive player is blocking the base or base path without the ball and the batter-runner is impeded.

**Whenever obstruction occurs**, whether or not a play is being made on a runner, the umpire shall declare obstruction and signal a delayed dead ball. The ball remains live. The obstructed runner is awarded the base they would have reached had obstruction not occurred. All other runners affected by the obstruction shall be awarded the bases they would have reached.

When an obstructed runner is awarded a base that they would have reached had obstruction not occurred, and a preceding runner is on that base, the obstructed runner shall be awarded that base and the runner occupying it is entitled to the next base without liability to be put out.

**Catcher Obstruction:** When catcher obstruction is called while the batter hits the ball and the batter-runner reaches first base safely and all other runners advance at least one base, the obstruction is cancelled. All action from the batted ball stands.

---

## RS #37 — Over-Running First Base

After over-running first base, the batter-runner may legally turn to their left or right when returning to first base. If any attempt is made to advance to second base (regardless of direction of turn), they may be liable to be put out. When using the double base, the batter-runner may use the white portion any time a play is not being made at first base on a batted ball. When the batter-runner overruns first base and returns to either portion of the base before an appeal is made, an appeal is not allowed.

---

## RS #38 — Overthrows

Runners are always awarded **two bases** on balls that are overthrown or become blocked as a result of hitting loose equipment belonging to the defensive team and should not be on the field (RS #17). Regardless of who made the throw, two bases are awarded from the last base legally touched at the time the thrown ball was released.

**Exception:** When the runner is returning to first base and the first throw is from the outfield, the award is one base from the last base touched at the time the ball goes out of play, and the runner is awarded home plate if between 1B and 2B and the ball goes out of play while in flight.

*When two runners are between the same bases at the time of the award, the award is determined by the position of the front runner.*

**Fast Pitch and Slow Pitch with Stealing:** On pitched balls that go out of play, runners are awarded one base from the last base touched at the time of the pitch. Should a batter receive a base on balls on the fourth ball and it gets away from the catcher and goes out of play, the award is first base only.

---

## RS #39 — Passing a Runner

Passing a runner may occur while runners are advancing or returning to a base. When a trail runner stops to see if a ball will be caught and the lead runner, also watching the ball, does not see the lead runner stop, the trail runner is called out and the ball remains live.

To pass a runner, the trail runner must pass the **entire body** of the lead runner, not just an arm or leg.

---

## RS #40 — Pitching (Fast Pitch)

Six basic features:

- **A. Contact with pitcher's plate** — Both feet must be on the ground in contact with the pitcher's plate. Shoulders in line with first and third base.
- **B. Signal** — Take a signal from the catcher while in contact with the pitcher's plate.
- **C. Preliminary to Delivery** — Bring hands together for 1–10 seconds. May step back with non-pivot foot to separate.
- **D. Start of Pitch** — Takes one hand off the ball.
- **E. Delivery** — May not be two full revolutions. The wrist shall be no farther from the body than the elbow. Must be released from below the hip. Continuous motion.
- **F. Step or Release** — One step only, forward toward home plate. Step must be simultaneous with release.
  - **Crow hop** (illegal): Sliding foot not in contact with plate; lifting and stepping forward; jumping forward with pivot foot.
  - **Leaping** (Women's/JO Fast Pitch — LEGAL if pivot foot toes pointed downward):  Both feet airborne on initial move is legal if toes of pivot foot are pointed downward. If toes are not pointed downward — illegal pitch.

**G.** Pitcher returning to pitch in same half-inning: no warm-up pitches. No limit on number of times returning to pitching position.

**H.** Approved drying agents may be used on the pitcher's hand (from the USA Softball list). Resin must be left on the ground.

---

## RS #41 — Pitcher's Uniform

A pitcher should be dressed identically to the other players on the team. A long-sleeve sweatshirt of any color is acceptable under the jersey, but sleeves may not extend past the wrist (a clear gap must be seen between the ball and the end of the shirt). If two or more players including the pitcher wear sweatshirts, they must be identical in color and style. No player may wear ragged, frayed, or slit sleeves on an exposed undershirt.

A pitcher may wear a batting glove and/or wristband on the glove hand and wrist, and may wear a pitcher's toe plate on their shoe.

**(Fast Pitch)** A pitcher may wear a band-aid on the finger or an arm wrap on the arm, as long as the umpire feels neither is distracting to the batter. Flesh-colored band-aids or wraps should be allowed. If a design or writing is on the wrap and the umpire feels it is distracting, the pitcher should be asked to remove it. The pitcher's glove/mitt may be of one solid color or multicolored as long as it does not contain colors that are the same as the color of the ball being used.

**(Slow Pitch)** A pitcher may wear a wristband on the pitching arm and tape on the pitching fingers, and may wear any color fielder's glove. A batting glove may not be worn on the pitching hand.

---

## RS #42 — Protested Game Upheld and Resumed

When a protest is upheld, the game shall be resumed from the point at which it was protested. The same line-ups must be used when the game is resumed, but there is no penalty for substitutions legally placed into the line-ups in the interim. Even if a player was not at the protested game, they are legal for substitution purposes when the game is rescheduled as long as they are on the roster at the time of the rescheduled game. If a player was ejected or disqualified in the original game after the protest was filed, that player may legally play in the rescheduled game because they were legally in the game at the time of the protest, unless the ejection drew a suspension for unsportsmanlike conduct.

---

## RS #43 — Run Scoring on the Third Out of an Inning

A run cannot score when the third out of an inning is:
- A putout of the batter-runner at first base (force out), or
- Another base if a preceding runner is forced because the batter becomes a batter-runner

Bases missed could result in a force out. On an appeal play, the force out is determined when the appeal is made, not when the infraction occurred. If the batter-runner or trail runner is put out prior to an appeal, the out on the appeal will not be considered a force out.

An appeal play on a runner leaving a base too soon on a caught fly ball is a time play, not a force. Any runs scored advance of the appealed runner and prior to the legal appeal count.

---

## RS #44 — Runner Hit by a Fair Batted Ball

**A. While in contact with the base:** A runner who is hit by a fair batted ball while in contact with the base should not be called out unless the act is intentional. The ball remains live or becomes immediately dead depending on the closest defensive player's position.

**B. While NOT in contact with the base:** A runner who is hit by a fair batted ball while NOT in contact with a base should be called out or ruled safe, depending on the interference rule (Rule 8, Section 7J & K or Rule 8, Section 8D-F).

---

## RS #45 — Runners Switching Positions on the Base Path

Following a "time out," should two or more base runners change positions on the base they originally held, the runners involved shall be called out and the manager of the offending team ejected.

---

## RS #46 — Shoes

Shoes in USA Softball do not include sandals or flip flops. Uppers must be solid and the toe must be closed. **Metal cleats are legal in Adult Fast Pitch and Slow Pitch, 14 & Under, 16 & Under, and 18 & Under JO Fast Pitch. They are NOT legal in 12 & Under JO Fast Pitch play.** Polyurethane or plastic cleats shaped to look like a metal triangle, toe heel or heel plate are illegal in youth. Metal cleats that screw into a post are illegal; metal cleats that screw into the shoe are legal (but still not allowed in 12U JO Girls Fast Pitch).

---

## RS #47 — Short-Handed Teams (Starting the Game)

All divisions of play may begin the game one player short:
- Slow Pitch and Coed: start with 9 players
- Fast Pitch: start with 8 players

When a team plays shorthanded, an out must be recorded in the last position in the batting order. In Coed, the out is in the 9th or 10th position depending on which gender is missing. If a player arrives after the game begins, that player must be placed in the last spot of the batting order.

In Slow Pitch, a team beginning with 9 players may not use the Shorthanded Rule and play with 8. In Fast Pitch, beginning with 8 players precludes using the Shorthanded Rule to play with 7.

---

## RS #48 — Short-Handed Teams (Continuing After Starting with Full Team)

After beginning with a full team, a team may continue with one player fewer than it used to begin the game, as long as the player vacancy is not created by ejection.

**A.** If a player is short because of disqualification, the game continues using the shorthanded rule. If already playing shorthanded and the disqualification occurs, the game is forfeited.

**B.** If a player leaving the game is a runner, the runner is called out even if the runner reached base safely.

**C.** Each time the absent player is due to bat, an out is recorded.

**D.** A player may not return to the lineup after playing shorthanded.
**Exception:** A player treated under the blood rule may return.

**E.** If an eligible substitute is available at the game or arrives before the game is over, the substitute MUST enter the game. Refusal makes the player ineligible for the remainder of the game.

**F.** A team may not bat less than 9 in Slow Pitch or 8 in Fast Pitch. The game is forfeited.

**G.** In Women's and Youth Fast Pitch, when a team playing shorthanded is involved in the tiebreaker, do not call an out — place on second base the player who precedes the absent player in the batting order.

---

## RS #49 — Sporting Behavior

The values of softball competition are based on good sporting behavior and fair play. Examples of unsporting behavior (not limited to):

**A.** Uniforms not worn properly (jerseys not tucked in, sleeves rolled under, caps worn backwards).

**B.** Exposed undergarments of different colors worn by different teammates on the same team.

**C.** Coaching tactics that endanger the safety of players.

**D.** After equipment check, illegal equipment being put back into play.

**E.** Use of equipment for other than its intended design.

**F.** The use of artificial noisemakers during championship play is prohibited.

---

## RS #50 — Stealing (Slow Pitch)

*Not applicable to Fast Pitch. Base stealing in Fast Pitch is governed by Rule 8, Section 4.*

---

## RS #51 — Substitutions

All substitutions must be reported to the plate umpire. A substitute is in the game when reported to the plate umpire. An unreported substitute is in the game after a pitch (legal or illegal) is thrown or a play has been made.

If an unreported substitute bats and it is discovered prior to the next pitch, all runners return to original bases, the unreported substitute is officially in the game and called out. If discovered on the last play of the game and before the umpires leave the field, runners return to original bases and the unreported substitute is officially in the game and called out.

Any player may be substituted for or replaced and re-enter once, as long as they occupy the same batting position whenever in the lineup.

A game is forfeited to a substitution violation when a player is removed by the umpire for using an illegal player, or if the ejection creates a situation where there are not enough players to continue.

---

## RS #52 — Throwing or Carrying a Bat

When a player throws the bat intentionally in anger, the player should be ejected. Should the bat slip from the batter's hands, there is no penalty unless the discarded bat prevents the defense from making a play on the ball, in which case interference should be ruled. There is no penalty when a player carries a bat to first base or any other base; however, should the player use the bat to prevent a defensive player from making a play, interference should be ruled.

---

## RS #53 — Tie Games or Games Called Which Are Less Than Regulation

When games that are called with less than regulation innings completed, or tied games, are rescheduled, the same procedure applies as in RS #42 (Protested Game Upheld and Resumed). In determining tie games after five innings have been played (a regulation game), the home team must have had the opportunity to bat and tie the score. If the home team has scored more runs than the visiting team and the game is called in the bottom of the fifth or sixth inning, the home team shall be the winner. If the visiting team has scored more runs than the home team in the sixth or seventh inning and the home team has not had the opportunity to complete its turn at bat, the game reverts to the previous inning. If that score was tied, it is a tie game. Should a game be called before five full innings are played (four and a half if the home team is ahead), the game should be resumed at the point it was called.

---

## RS #54 — Tie Breaker Rule (Women's, Girls' JO Fast Pitch, and Men's Senior Slow Pitch)

During each half-inning of the inning used to enforce the tiebreaker, the offensive team shall begin its turn at bat with the player scheduled to bat last in that respective half-inning being placed at second base. For example, if the number five batter is to lead off, the number four batter in the batting order should be placed on second base. A substitute may be inserted for the runner.

It is the responsibility of the umpire and scorer to notify the teams as to which player starts the half inning at second base. Should the wrong player be placed on second base and it is brought to the umpire's attention, there is no penalty — correct the error and place the correct person on the base. This should occur even if a pitch has been thrown, or the runner has advanced a base. An unreported substitute would be legally in the game after a pitch has been thrown or a play has been made.

When a team playing shorthanded is involved in the tiebreaker and it is the shorthanded spot in the batting order that should start at second base, do not call an out. Instead, place on second base the player who precedes the absent player in the batting order.

In scoring, the run scored by a player starting as a runner at second base shall be charged to the defensive team and not the pitcher.

*For 12U JO Girls and 16U/18U Gold Fast Pitch Pool Play, there will be a time limit of 1 hour 20 minutes to finish the inning, then one additional inning. The 10U Championship and "IF" game will have a 2-hour time limit. 12U, 14U, 16U, and 18U will not have a time limit.*

---

## RS #55 — Wheel Chair Use

Under the ADA ruling of July 25, 1990, wheel chairs are allowed on the playing field. Players while batting, running bases, or playing defense, coaches in coaching boxes, and umpires behind the plate or on the bases may use wheel chairs.
