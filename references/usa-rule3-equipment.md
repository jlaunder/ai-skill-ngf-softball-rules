# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 3: Equipment

*Rule 3 governs equipment for USA Softball Championship Play. USA Softball certification marks indicate that equipment has been tested and complies with USA Softball's rules; they are not a warranty of safety.*

---

## Section 1: The Official Bat

**A. Certified/Approved:** The official bat must meet all USA Softball specifications and:
1. Be free of burrs, dents, cracks, sharp edges, rattles and show no signs of excessive wear. No more than 34 inches long, no more than 38 ounces in weight, no more than 2.250 inches in diameter at its largest part (including tolerance for expansion). Must bear the appropriate USA Softball certification mark and must not be on the USA Softball Non-Approved Bat List.
2. Must be included on the list of approved bat models published by USA Softball. OR
3. Must, in the sole opinion of the umpire, have been manufactured prior to 2000 and if tested, would comply with the USA Softball Bat Performance Standard. This includes wooden bats.
4. **Barrel Compression Testing:** A USA Softball approved barrel compression testing machine shall be used at all National Championship Finals, coordinated by the Tournament Director under the direction of the umpire staff.

*Exception: Any bats bearing USA Softball, USSSA, or WBSC Certification marks that pass Fast Pitch Compression testing thresholds are allowed in USA Softball Men's Major and Men's Open Fast Pitch Championships.*

**B. Safety Knob:** The knob shall have a diameter of at least 1.6 inches with no sharp edges. The knob must be permanently attached to the bat and may be covered with grip tape. The use of an internal or external bat sensor is allowed provided the sensor is properly attached. *(2026 Rule Change 3-1B)*

**C. Handle:** The region of the bat from the knob (not including the knob) to the start of the taper where the diameter increases. The handle includes a grip.

**D. Grip:** The handle region must have a grip made of cork, grip tape, or other synthetic material. No bare metal, wood or composite material may be exposed in this area. The grip shall be no less than 6 inches and no more than 15 inches from the knob. Resin, pine tar or spray substances are permissible on the grip region only.

**E. Taper:** The transition area between the handle and the barrel. The taper shall have a generally conical shape.

**F. Barrel:** The region from the taper to the end cap. The barrel shall be smooth and round within 0.050 inches in diameter.

**G. End Cap:** The cap at the barrel end of the bat. The end cap shall be permanently affixed to prevent alterations to the interior of the bat and cannot be removed without damaging or destroying the end cap or barrel.

**H. Altered Bat:** A bat's characteristics shall remain fixed at the time of manufacture and may not be altered in any way. Approved bats shall be considered altered if they are rolled, shaved, repainted, weighted or modified to change characteristics from those produced by the manufacturer. Identifying the bat by laser marking, engraving, or painting the player's name or number does not make the bat altered.

**I. Construction:** The official bat shall be of one-piece construction, multi-piece permanently assembled, or two-piece interchangeable construction. Interchangeable bats must meet additional criteria:
1. Mating components must have a unique locking key to prevent uncertified combinations.
2. All component combinations must meet one-piece bat rules.
3. Manufacturer must demonstrate that the bat fails in a safe manner.
4. Bat components must be designed to prevent uncertified alterations.

*Note: If the bat is not constructed of wood and not made of one-piece construction with the barrel end closed, the barrel end shall have a rubber, vinyl plastic, or other approved material insert or end cap.*

## Section 2: Warm-Up Bat

The warm-up bat shall meet the following requirements:

**A.** Stamped with 1/4 inch letters "WB" on either end, or marked "WARM-UP BAT" in one-inch letters on the barrel end only.

**B.** Must comply with the safety grip and safety knob requirements of the official bat.

**C.** Must be of one-piece construction or a one-piece permanently assembled bat that clearly distinguishes itself as a warm-up bat and is approved by USA Softball.

**D.** The knob shall have a diameter of at least 1.6 inches with no sharp edges and must be permanently attached.

**E.** Only approved attachments included on the list published by USA Softball can be used on warm-up bats.

## Section 3: The Official Softball

**A. Certified/Approved:** Only softballs bearing an ASA or USA Softball approved certification mark signifying compliance with Ball COR and Ball Compression limitations then in effect will be allowed. The ball must not appear on the USA Softball Non-Approved Ball List.

USA Softball certification marks by color:
- **Green:** Ball Compression 300 lbs or less, Ball COR .520 or under
- **Red (Max 300):** Ball Compression 300 lbs or less, Ball COR .470 or under
- **Red (275–375):** Ball Compression 275.0–375.0 lbs, Ball COR .470 or under
- **Black (Max 300):** Ball Compression 300 lbs or less, Ball COR .440 or under
- **Black (275–375):** Ball Compression 275.0–375.0 lbs, Ball COR .440 or under
- **Blue (Max 300):** Ball Compression 300 lbs or less, Ball COR .400 or under
- **Blue (275–375):** Ball Compression 275.0–375.0 lbs, Ball COR .400 or under

**B. Cover:** The official softball shall have a cover of leather or synthetic material. The ball shall be regular, smooth-seamed, and smooth-surfaced.

**C. The 16-Inch Ball:** At least 16.0 inches in circumference (measured across two seams), at least 9.0 ounces. White cover, white stitches, Ball COR .470 or under, Ball Compression 375.0 lbs or under. Used in: All Men's 16-inch Slow Pitch.

**D. The 14-Inch Ball:** At least 14.0 inches in circumference, at least 8.0 ounces.

**E. The 12-Inch Ball:** 11⅞–12¼ inches in circumference (measured across two seams), 6.50–7.00 ounces. No fewer than 88 stitches per cover, sewn by the two-needle method.
1. **Fast Pitch Ball:** Yellow optic cover, red stitch, Ball COR .470 or under, Ball Compression 275.0–375.0 lbs. Used in:
   - Women's and Women's Masters Fast Pitch, Girls 12U, 14U, 16U, 18U Fast Pitch
   - Men's, Men's Masters, Men's Seniors Fast Pitch, Boys 12U, 14U, 16U, 18U Fast Pitch (may have white cover, white stitches)
2. **Modified Pitch Ball:** White cover, red stitch or yellow optic cover, red stitch, Ball COR .470 or under, Ball Compression 275.0–375.0 lbs.
3. **Slow Pitch Ball:** Yellow optic cover, red stitch, size 11.875 inches or over, 6.250 ounces or over, COR .520 or under, Compression 300.0 lbs or under, Dynamic Stiffness 5500.0 lbs or under.

**F. The 11-Inch Ball:** At least 11.0 inches in circumference, at least 6.0 ounces. No fewer than 80 stitches per cover, sewn by the two-needle method.
1. **Fast Pitch Ball:** White cover/white stitch or optic yellow cover/red stitch (may be white stitch for Boys Fast Pitch), Ball COR .470 or under, Ball Compression 375.0 lbs or under. Used in: Boys 10U Fast Pitch, Girls 10U Fast Pitch.
2. **Slow Pitch Ball:** Yellow optic cover, red stitch, Ball COR .520 or under, Ball Compression 300.0 lbs or under. Used in: Women's Slow Pitch, Coed (female batters only), Women's Masters and Seniors Slow Pitch, Boys 10U and 12U Slow Pitch, Girls 10U/12U/14U/16U/18U Slow Pitch.

**G. The 10-Inch Ball:** At least 10.0 inches in circumference, at least 5.50 ounces. Used in 6U and 8U play.

## Section 4: Glove/Mitt

A glove/mitt may be worn by any player. The dimensions of any glove/mitt used by any fielder shall not exceed the specifications below:

| Measurement | Maximum |
|---|---|
| A. Palm width (top) | 8 in. |
| B. Palm width (bottom) | 8½ in. |
| C. Top opening of web | 5 in. |
| D. Bottom opening of web | 4½ in. |
| E. Web top to bottom | 7¼ in. |
| F. 1st finger crotch seam | 7½ in. |
| G. Thumb crotch seam | 7½ in. |
| H. Crotch seam | 17½ in. |
| I. Thumb top to bottom edge | 9¼ in. |
| J. 1st finger top to bottom edge | 14 in. |
| K. 2nd finger top to bottom edge | 13¼ in. |
| L. 3rd finger top to bottom edge | 12¼ in. |
| M. 4th finger top to bottom edge | 11 in. |

**(Fast Pitch)** The pitcher's glove/mitt may be of one solid color or multicolored as long as the color(s) are not the same color as the ball being used in the game.

## Section 5: Catcher's Equipment, Face Masks/Guards, and Helmets

**A. Catcher Masks (Adult Fast Pitch):** Catchers must wear masks with throat protectors. An extended wire protector may be worn in lieu of an attached throat protector. Helmets are optional; if worn, any style is allowed. Fast Pitch catchers cannot merely wear the plastic face mask/guard.

**B. Catcher Masks (Junior Olympic Slow Pitch):** Catchers must wear an approved batter's helmet with ear flaps, the catcher's helmet and mask, or an approved plastic face mask/guard with catcher's helmet. The throat protector is optional on the catcher's mask.

**C. Catcher Masks & Equipment (Junior Olympic Fast Pitch):** Catchers must wear a mask with throat protector, approved helmet with ear flaps, shin guards which offer protection to the knee caps, and a body protector. An extended wire protector may be worn in lieu of an attached throat protector. Any player warming up a Junior Olympic pitcher must wear a mask with throat protector and approved helmet with ear flaps. Fast Pitch catchers cannot merely wear the plastic face mask/guard.

*Note (Sections A–C): The ice hockey goalie style face mask is approved for use by catchers. If the throat protection built into the mask is inadequate in the umpire's judgment, a throat protector must be added before using.*

**D. Face Masks/Guards:** Any defensive or offensive player may wear a face mask/guard. Face masks/guards that are cracked, deformed, or missing padding are prohibited.

*Exception: Fast Pitch catchers cannot merely wear the plastic face mask/guard — they must wear the full protective equipment required by Sections A–C.*

*Note: A NOCSAE approved mask is only compatible with certain NOCSAE approved helmets. Verify proper compatibility with the manufacturer.*

**E. Helmets:**

1. **Offense:** All Adult Fast Pitch, Modified Pitch and all Junior Olympic offensive players (including the on-deck batter, and JO players acting as coaches in the coach's box) must properly wear double ear flap NOCSAE approved batting helmets. All JO batting helmets may be equipped with chin straps. **All Junior Olympic Fast Pitch batting helmets shall be equipped with a securely fastened NOCSAE approved face mask/guard.** Batting helmets that are broken, cracked, dented, or illegally altered are prohibited.

   *Effect: Failure to wear the batting helmet when ordered by the umpire shall cause the player to be removed from the game. Wearing the helmet improperly or removing the helmet during a live ball play, if judged by the umpire to be a deliberate act, shall cause the violator to be declared out immediately. The ball remains live. Calling a runner out for removing a helmet does not remove force play situations.*

2. **Defense:** Any defensive player may wear a helmet, with or without ear flaps, of similar color as the team caps. It must have a bill. *Exception: Hockey Style Face Mask.*

**(Fast Pitch Only)** Helmets shall have a non-glare (not mirror-like) surface.

**F. Protective Equipment:** Regardless of whether helmets are mandated by rule, any player has the right to wear a helmet at any time during any part of the game.

## Section 6: Uniform

All players on a team shall properly wear uniforms that are like in color and style. All protective equipment should be worn properly. If a player refuses to remove jewelry, illegal shoes or illegal parts of the uniform when requested by the umpire, the player will not be allowed to play.

*Exception: Men's D and E/Rec Slow Pitch, Women's Open, C, D and E/Rec Slow Pitch and Coed C, D and E/Rec Slow Pitch: the only uniform requirement are shirts similar in color with a number.*

**A. Headwear:**
1. **(Fast Pitch Male)** Ball caps are mandatory, must be alike, and worn properly.
2. **(Female Fast Pitch and all Slow Pitch)** Ball caps, visors, and head/sweat bands are optional for players. If worn, they may be mixed and must be worn properly. Colors may include black, white, gray, beige, pink or team colors. Handkerchiefs do not qualify as headwear and cannot be worn around the head or neck. Plastic visors are not allowed.

**B. Pants/Sliding Pants:** All players' pants may be long, short, or mixed in style, as long as they are like in color. Players may wear a solid-colored pair of sliding pants. If more than one player wears them, they must be like in color and style. No player may wear ragged, frayed or slit legs on exposed sliding pants.

**C. Undershirts:** Players may wear a solid-colored undershirt. If more than one player wears one, they must be like in color and style. No player may wear ragged, frayed or slit sleeves on exposed undershirts.

**D. Numbers:** An Arabic whole number (0–99) of contrasting color, or outlined in contrasting color, at least three inches high must be clearly visible on all uniform shirts. No players on the same team may wear identical numbers. Players without numbers will not be permitted to play. (00 and 0, 01 and 1 are examples of different numbers.)

**E. Casts/Prosthesis:** All casts and splints must be padded. Prostheses may be worn. Braces with exposed hard surfaces must be padded. Any equipment judged by the umpire to be potentially dangerous is illegal.

**F. Jewelry:** Exposed jewelry judged by the umpire to be dangerous must be removed and may not be worn during the game. Medical alert bracelets or necklaces are not considered jewelry; if worn, they must be taped to the body so the medical alert information remains visible.

**G. Shoes:** Must be worn by all participants. Shoes must have canvas, leather, or similar material uppers with closed toes. Soles may be smooth or have soft or hard rubber cleats. Ordinary metal sole or heel plates may be used if cleats do not extend more than ¾ of an inch from the sole or heel. Shoes with round metal spikes are illegal. Shoes with detachable cleats that screw ON are not permitted; shoes with detachable cleats that screw INTO the shoe are permitted.

Metal cleat rules by division:
- **Legal:** Adult Fast Pitch, Adult Slow Pitch, 14U, 16U, and 18U JO Fast Pitch
- **Illegal:** JO Fast Pitch 10U and 12U, JO Slow Pitch, Coed Slow Pitch, Senior Slow Pitch. No metal spikes nor hard plastic or polyurethane spikes similar to metal sole and heel plates are allowed.

*Effect: Players wearing metal cleats after being warned by the umpire shall be ejected.*

---

## Section 7: All Equipment

**A.** USA Softball reserves the right to withhold or withdraw approval of any equipment which significantly changes the character of the game, affects the safety of participants or spectators, or renders a player's performance more a product of equipment than individual skill. The characteristics of any approved equipment cannot be unnaturally changed. Examples include icing, cooling, or heating of any equipment. Warming devices for bats are not approved.

**B.** The National Championship UIC, Assistant UIC or Staff has the right to remove any equipment not meeting Rule 3 (including bats and helmets) through pregame inspection, bat testing, or during a game. Removed equipment will be returned after the team's final game of the Championship.

**C. (JO Fast Pitch Only)** For pregame inspection, all equipment shall be inspected by the umpire and is to be placed outside the dugout/team area prior to the start of the game.
