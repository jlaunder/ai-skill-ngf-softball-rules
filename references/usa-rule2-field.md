# USA Softball 2026 — Rule 2: The Playing Field

---

## Section 1: Playing Field

The playing field is the area within which the ball may be legally played and fielded. It is divided into fair territory and foul territory.

## Section 2: Ground Rules

Ground rules shall be established by the home team, subject to the approval of the umpire. Ground rules may modify the general rules only if the general rules specifically allow such modification. Ground rules may not modify rules that affect the fundamental nature of the game.

## Section 3: Field Dimensions

### A. Pitching Distances (from front of pitcher's plate to back of home plate)

| Division | Distance |
|---|---|
| Junior Olympic Girls Fast Pitch (12U) | **40 feet** |
| Junior Olympic Girls Fast Pitch (14U) | 43 feet |
| Junior Olympic Boys Fast Pitch | 46 feet |
| Women's Fast Pitch | 43 feet |
| Men's Fast Pitch | 46 feet |

### B. Base Distances

| Division | Base Path |
|---|---|
| Junior Olympic Girls Fast Pitch (all ages) | **60 feet** |
| Men's/Women's Fast Pitch | 60 feet |

### C. Batter's Box

- Two batter's boxes, one on each side of home plate
- Each box: 7 feet long × 3 feet wide
- Inside edge of the batter's box is 6 inches from home plate
- The lines are considered inside the batter's box

### D. Catcher's Box

- Located directly behind home plate
- 8 feet long × 10 feet wide (measured from the back of home plate)
- The catcher must remain in the catcher's box until the pitch is released (Fast Pitch)

### E. Coach's Box

- Located outside the diamond near first base and third base
- Coaches must remain in the coach's box during play

### F. On-Deck Circle

- A circle 5 feet in diameter located in foul territory
- One on each side of home plate, near each dugout
- The on-deck batter must remain in the on-deck circle until it is their turn to bat

### G. Home Plate

- A 5-sided slab of whitened rubber
- 17 inches wide
- 8.5 inches on each side parallel to the baselines
- 12 inches along each of the two sides extending toward the pitcher
- Located at the intersection of the baselines

### H. Double Base (First Base Safety Base)

- First base shall be a double base placed so that the white portion is in fair territory and the colored portion extends into foul territory
- Each portion is 15 inches × 15 inches
- **Batter-runner rule:** On a play at first base, the batter-runner may use either portion of the base when first arriving. If a play is being made on the batter-runner at first base on a batted ball, the batter-runner must use the colored (foul territory) portion of the double base. The fielder must use the white (fair territory) portion.
- After safely reaching first base, the runner uses only the white (fair territory) portion of the base
- On an errant or missed throw that pulls the fielder into foul territory, the batter-runner may touch the white portion

### F. Pitcher's Plate

- A rubber slab: 24 inches long × 6 inches wide
- The front of the pitcher's plate shall be a specified distance from the back of home plate (see distances above)
- The pitcher's plate shall be elevated at least 6 inches above home plate

### Commitment Line / Scoring Line (Fast Pitch)

- A **commitment line** is drawn through fair territory, 8 feet from home plate, extending from the back corner of home plate to the edge of the playing field in foul territory
- A **scoring line / home plate extension** extends from the front corner of home plate in foul territory

Once a runner crosses or touches the commitment line, the runner is committed to scoring and must proceed home. If the runner is tagged while between the commitment line and home plate, they are out. If the catcher blocks the home plate area (scoring line), obstruction may be called.

### Three-Foot Running Lane

- An area extending from halfway between home plate and first base to first base
- The lane is 3 feet wide to the foul territory side of the baseline
- The inside line of the three-foot lane is an extension of the first base foul line
- Batter-runner must run within the three-foot lane when a play is being made at first base with a thrown ball
- Lines of the lane are considered inside the lane (i.e., batter-runner may run on the lines)
